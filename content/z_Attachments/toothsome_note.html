A figurine, carved black, the size of a fist. It rests beneath the rubble in the upper loft of Chalet Brantifax — a highland estate two days north of Wytchway. You will know it by smell before you see it.

Take it. Tell no one. Leave it wrapped in cloth at the base of the broken fountain in Rivington, south Baldurs Gate, before the next new moon.

A second bag — three times this one — will be waiting beneath the stone.

Ask no questions. We are watching to see that you don't.

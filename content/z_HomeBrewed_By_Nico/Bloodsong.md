# Bloodsong, +2 Battleaxe
*Weapon (battleaxe), very rare (requires attunement by a creature proficient with martial weapons)*

## Picture
![[Bloodsong.png|300]]

## Description
**Bloodsong** is a heavy battleaxe forged of blackened steel, its surface engraved with faintly glowing runes that pulse when the weapon draws blood. When swung, the axe emits a deep, resonant hum that rises in pitch the closer it comes to striking true. The sound is not loud, but carries a strange, musical tone — a weapon that sings for battle.

## Stats
- **Weapon Type:** [[battleaxe-xphb|Battleaxe]] 
- **Damage:** [[Mechanics/CLI/rules/item-properties.md#Versatile|Versatile]]
	- One-handed: 1d8 slashing 
	- Two-handed: 1d10 slashing   
- **Rarity:** Very rare  
- **Requires Attunement**  
- **Bonus:** You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon.

## Magic Property — Blood Requiem
When you reduce a hostile creature to **0 hit points** with **Bloodsong**, the axe releases a low, harmonic tone that lingers in the air for a heartbeat. You may choose one of the following effects:

- **Reaper’s Respite:** You gain **temporary hit points** equal to your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus (minimum 1).  
- **Unending Strike:** You can immediately make **one additional melee attack** as part of the same action, targeting another creature within range.  

Once you use this property, you cannot use it again until the start of your next turn.

## Flavor Note
When the weapon tastes fresh blood, its runes brighten, and the faint hum becomes a steady, melodic vibration in the wielder’s grip — as though the axe itself is singing in satisfaction.

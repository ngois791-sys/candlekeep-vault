### 1. Joy of Extradimensional Spaces ✅ Done

**Level:** 1 | **Act:** One | **Status:** Complete

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** The sage Matreous discovered a gateway to the permanent extradimensional mansion of the mage Fistandia, hidden inside Candlekeep in a private study room. He found the command word ("scepter") in Fistandia's margin notes and stepped inside. The party enters, explores the mansion, and solves a puzzle (seven lettered books spelling "liberty") to reopen the exit portal. When Matreous exits carrying an imp figurine, the enchantment breaks and the imp kills him. The party fights the invisible imp in the study.

**Key Elements:**

- Fistandia's Mansion — permanent Mordenkainen's Magnificent Mansion; puzzle books spell LIBERTY; animated broom, swarm of books, two faerie dragons, mimic chair, animated chained library
- Imp figurine — carved onyx; actually a real imp Fistandia trapped in statuette form; enchantment breaks when removed from the mansion; kills Matreous the instant he exits
- Homunculi Cumin and Coriander — can speak Common; acquired by the party
- Matreous — neutral human sage; killed by imp on exit; cannot be revived (yet)
- Fistandia and her colleague Freyot are still alive somewhere — their homunculi prove this (a homunculus dies when its master dies)
- Command word to open portal from outside: "scepter" | Command word from inside: "liberty"

**What Happened (Your Campaign):** Party rescued Matreous, explored the mansion, acquired Cumin and Coriander. Matreous killed by imp on exit. Bounty reward collected from the Avowed.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Subtle (Retroactive)** A Keeper agent identified something in Fistandia's margin notes — a reference pointing toward ancient planar texts about what lies beneath Candlekeep. Matreous was directed toward the book through irregular channels so he would find his way into the mansion and recover those references. When he was killed before sharing his findings, the agent had to begin again.

> [!note] How to Surface This Much later, Pell flags that the copy request that led Matreous to Fistandia's book came through irregular channels. Thessaly confirms the text has since been quietly reclassified. The pieces only click in Act Two or Three — but when they do, the party realises their first bounty was already part of this.

### 2. Mazfroth's Mighty Digressions ✅ Done

**Level:** 2 | **Act:** One | **Status:** Complete

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** Books from Amberdune Books in Baldur's Gate are arriving at Candlekeep as gingwatzims — shapeshifting ectoplasmic creatures mimicking objects. When one attacks the party, they investigate. Amberdune Books is run by a pack of jackalweres loyal to the deceased Nidalia (your campaign: a sphinx; published: a lamia). The pack, led by Korvala, has been selling gingwatzim-books to raise money for Nidalia's resurrection. Nidalia's heart is hidden in their Blackgate hideout.

**Key Elements:**

- Gingwatzims — ectoplasm spheres that mimic objects; must revert to true form to feed on life energy; communicate by telepathy; Korvala knows the creation ritual (taught by Nidalia)
- Amberdune Books — in the Wide, Upper City of Baldur's Gate; two jackalweres per shift
- Amberdune Pack — seven jackalweres: Korvala (leader), Avani, Inbar, Marliza, Ramah, Theryn, Zan
- Hidden vault under Korvala's office — Nidalia's heart (black silk, atop 450 gp); the real books; Castanamir's Guide to Gingwatzims
- Real books in vault: Mazfroth's Mighty Digressions, The Dark Hunger, Fallen Tethyamar, Castanamir's Guide to Gingwatzims, Wanderings of a Humble Aasimar, Daughters of Graz'zt (on lamia origins)
- Rug of smothering in common room; mimic disguised as a trunk in storage
- Reward from Avowed for returning real books: helm of comprehending languages

**IMPORTANT:** Amberdune Books is entirely Nidalia's operation — the jackalweres sold gingwatzims to raise resurrection money. This has nothing to do with the Keepers of Forbidden Lore.

**What Happened (Your Campaign):** Party discovered Amberdune Books, got a "weird vibe" from workers, watched the shift change. Luci followed them, infiltrated, stole gold, books, and Nidalia's heart. Party negotiated with Korvala, helped with the resurrection ritual. Nidalia resurrected — boons granted, memories taken.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Subtle (Retroactive)** A Keeper agent had separately been trying to acquire Castanamir's Guide to Gingwatzims — a rare text on what gingwatzims are and how they function. They enquired at Amberdune Books as a potential source for unusual texts. The enquiry was declined. The agent never made meaningful contact with the jackalweres. If the party ever returns and digs through old Amberdune correspondence, they find a letter bearing the Keeper seal enquiring about the text.

> [!note] How to Surface This The subtlest breadcrumb of all. When the party later recognises the Keeper seal, they might think back to the Amberdune visit and wonder. It's fine if they never make the connection.

### 3. Book of the Raven 🔄 Current

**Level:** 3 | **Act:** One | **Status:** In Progress

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** The Book of the Raven is a firsthand account of Vistani travel; inside it, founding Scarlet Sash member Anil Zasperdes hid a treasure map leading to Chalet Brantifax — a remote highland estate used by wereravens. Four wereravens currently squat there. A shadow crossing in the graveyard leads to a Shadowfell necropolis with a wight and ghouls.

**Key Elements:**

- Book of the Raven — thin, black oak covers pecked by ravens; 33 pages of Vistani travel lore; map hidden inside by Anil Zasperdes
- Four wereravens: Madrina Natterask, Taspar Hatchhill, Rennick Groka, Vinique
- Chalet Brantifax key locations: C9 (graveyard), C3 (haunted well), C14 (baron's loft), C15 (attic nursery)
- Orcus Figurine — in C14 (baron's loft) under rubble, hidden by Vinique. Carved from a petrified ogre's heart. Smells of decay within 5 ft. Properties: prevents undead within 30 ft from being turned; prevents raising the dead within 30 ft; can summon a wraith-like avatar of Orcus
- Shadow crossing — through Sylphene's open grave in C9 at night; leads to a Shadowfell necropolis
- Shadowfell necropolis — gargoyles, 12 ghouls rising from open graves, wight Drovath Harrn (wears Ring of Jumping)
- A raven with the restless soul of a dead girl named Heluthe may deliver the book to the party

**Bhaal Hooks — All Four Confirmed:**

- C9 (graveyard) | C3 (haunted well) | C14 (baron's loft, Orcus Figurine) | C15 (attic nursery) See [[Lucifurry — Bhaal Arc Tracker]] for full scripting. Plan Hooks 5 and 6 (First Bloodthirst, Drovath's Whisper) for the Shadowfell necropolis session.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Subtle (Plant This Session)** A Keeper agent visited Chalet Brantifax recently, specifically hunting the Orcus Figurine. They believed its properties — preventing undead from being turned and raised — could neutralise Miirym's spectral nature in the catacombs. They searched C14 but the wereravens had already secured the figurine. The agent left empty-handed.

> - A crowbar that clearly doesn't belong to the wereravens
> 
> Don't draw attention to it. Let it sit.

> [!important] Personal Arc — Lucifurry ⭐ BHAAL HOOKS Plan Hooks 5 and 6 for the Shadowfell necropolis. See [[Lucifurry — Bhaal Arc Tracker]].

### 4. A Deep and Creeping Darkness

**Level:** 4 | **Act:** One | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** A mining company hires the party to investigate Vermeillon, a mountain village abandoned 70 years ago after a platinum mine explosion. The real cause: meenlocks spontaneously manifested from the extreme fear of miners trapped in the collapse, psychically tormented the survivors, and kidnapped them one by one, transforming them into more meenlocks. The mayor (Lei Duvezin) was the last taken and now wanders the mansion as a meenlock. The party must explore the village, find the meenlock lair in the mine, and clear it out.

**Key Elements:**

- Vermeillon — abandoned mountain village; 3 days ride from Maerin (nearest town at mountain base)
- Mine explosion 70 years ago — 60 miners underground; 30+ died in collapse; 16 rescued; 11 never found (became meenlocks)
- Meenlocks — corrupted fey; sensitive to bright light; use psychic torment and hallucinations; transform humanoids via hours/days of torment; spontaneously arise from extreme fear in places where the Feywild's influence is strong; can't be controlled, only destroyed
- Mayor Lei Duvezin — dark-skinned female dwarf; transformed into a meenlock; still wanders the mayor's mansion; wears a platinum signet ring (snowdrop blossom, 75 gp)
- Mayor's mansion library (H9) — contains her journal documenting the disaster and the disappearances; she had found a book on meenlocks and written "Could this be the answer?" in it — she figured it out just before being taken
- Key NPCs in Maerin: Lukas Grosvenor (human tavernkeeper, 95, former miner; asks party to lay flowers on wife Lorna's grave and retrieve her necklace from a hollow tree in the village center); Astra Vorn (half-elf florist, 78; fled as a child; vague memories)
- Meenlock lair in the mine — M5 (entrance), M6 (Chamber of Weeping with mossy tentacles), M7 (Mossy Maze for ambushes), M9 (Transformation Chamber — stone slabs, dynamite trap on four pillars)
- Feywild crossing — shallow lake on the mountainside above the lair; becomes a fey crossing during the full moon (anyone submerging surfaces in the Feywild)

> [!important] Personal Arc — Ash The full moon transforms the lake above the meenlock lair into a Feywild crossing. Ash cannot use magic during a full moon. If the party arrives during or near a full moon, this adventure puts his secret in direct geographical relevance. Plan accordingly.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Subtle** Before the meenlocks drove everyone out, the Keepers used Vermeillon as a discreet waystation for moving acquired texts away from Candlekeep undetected. The creature activity disrupted their operation before they could retrieve everything — materials were left hidden.

> [!note] How to Plant It In the mayor's mansion library or a locked room in the mine office: a hidden compartment with empty book-transport crates (cloth-lined) and one old water-damaged Keeper document referencing "a shipment of texts from the Keep" and a contact titled only "the Keeper." The party won't understand this yet. First physical Keeper trace they've ever found.

### 5. Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme

**Level:** 4 | **Act:** One | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** The party wakes with a sinister rhyme stuck in their heads. The adventure takes place entirely in Candlekeep's Firefly Cellar — a windowless basement sealed in quarantine. Six events unfold in escalating order: quarantine, Ebder's possessed outburst, discovery of Yowen Pilt's singing skull, K'Tulah's escape attempt, Shemshime possessing characters to attack each other, and the final confrontation. The book is held by the kenku caretaker Crinkle. The music box is broken and skips — the final stanza (which reveals how to defeat Shemshime: crush it under 1,000+ lbs) can't be heard until the box is repaired.

**Key Elements:**

- Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme — gnomish mechanical book, wood and copper cover, 18" square; clockwork pop-up watermill with animated family-death scenes; music box built into spine is broken (cylinder sits askew, skips back before the final stanza); indestructible (AC 14, 15 hp, damage threshold 15)
- The rhyme/curse — spreads by being heard; resist by concentrating as if on a spell; a remove curse suppresses it on all creatures for 10 minutes; silence spell negates it in the area
- Shemshime — malevolent spirit attached to the watermill family story; a gnome bard accidentally created a conduit for it by writing the book; can possess characters (DC 15 Cha save or attack allies for 1 minute)
- To defeat Shemshime: repair music box (3 successful DC 15 Dex checks, each requires DC 13 Int save or 4d6 psychic damage), hear the final stanza, lure Shemshime under something weighing 1,000+ lbs — the 1,500 lb stone flying-book sculpture suspended above the stacks by three chains (5 hp each)
- Crinkle — chaotic neutral kenku, 55; Firefly Cellar caretaker; loves curios; has ring of the ram; found the book during a restoration project and hid it in her quarters
- Quarantine occupants: Varnyr (sun elf senior scribe, 685; calm but sharp), Ebder Smallstone (human scribe, 33; pessimist, widower), Gailby Smallstone (his 8-year-old daughter; curious, was the one who spread the rhyme), K'Tulah (tabaxi druid scholar, 30; hates confinement)
- Yowen Pilt's skull — hidden in a secret floor compartment in the firefly room (F4); bones of Pilt and the Avowed who killed him to stop the curse 600 years ago; skull sings when the curse gets strong enough
- Reward: bag of holding from the Avowed

> [!important] Personal Arc — Morvax A sound in your head you cannot stop. That compels you whether you want it to or not. The rhyme is an externalised, weaponised version of what Morvax experiences every day with the whispering relics. Let Kade sit with that during the quarantine — especially during the Puppets event, when the sound makes people hurt each other.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Subtle** The book was deliberately introduced to Candlekeep by a Keeper agent as a planned weapon — to incapacitate the Avowed during a text heist that ultimately never happened (timing failed). The book was already inside before the plan fell through. Tucked in a seam of the book's protective case is an old Keeper delivery note.

> [!note] How to Plant It Investigation DC 14 to find while examining the protective case. Confirms the book was placed deliberately, that someone inside Candlekeep accepted the delivery, and that the intent was to "create a window" for an operation. No names. First hint of a Keeper inside contact.

### 6. The Price of Beauty

**Level:** 5 | **Act:** One | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** The Price of Beauty has a magic mirror set into its cover. When someone looks into it and expresses unhappiness or a desire for change, an image of Sune appears and a portal opens to the Temple of the Restful Lily — a real physical temple in the High Forest (not a demiplane), southeast of Silverymoon, built by the book's author Sylvarie Silversong over a fey-enchanted hot spring. Ten years ago a coven of three green hags (the Fetid Gaze) tricked Sylvarie into a cursed portrait bargain, transformed her into a medusa, and imprisoned her in the basement shrine. They now run the bathhouse using illusory disguises as three elf sisters. The hags offer magical transformations to wealthy guests via cursed portraits — benefits last one year, then catastrophically invert when renounced or when the year expires.

**Key Elements:**

- The Price of Beauty — soft green leather, scent of lilies, polished oval magic mirror in cover; author: Sylvarie Silversong; portal activates when the holder expresses desire for change while gazing at the mirror; leads to the Temple of the Restful Lily in the High Forest (physical location, accessed via portal)
- Missing Avowed acolyte: Falthrax Loderr — shield dwarf, devotee of Sune; renounced his bargain early; now aged to venerable years, in pain, serving the hags in the tower kitchen
- The Fetid Gaze — three green hags:
    - Dread Morgan (disguised as moon elf Morganna): knows the binding ritual — scroll hidden in a bone ornament in her hair
    - Vile Sazha (disguised as sun elf Azirssa): knows the canvas-making method — tattooed on her withered arm in Infernal; terrified of fire; hates dwarves
    - Auntie Greenbones (disguised as wood elf Greensong, feigns blindness): knows the demon-ichor paint recipe — inscribed inside Morty's collar (Morty is her hell hound disguised as a mastiff)
- Cursed portrait bargain — the hags DECEIVE guests; clients don't know the true cost. Three possible benefits: ability score +2, physical transformation, or reduced age. Benefit lasts 1 year. Renouncing triggers the curse immediately; waiting the year also triggers it: the benefit inverts catastrophically. Portraits painted on humanoid skin with demon-ichor paint — impervious to damage unless disrupted (DC 18 Str Athletics, or splashed with celestial blood). Compulsion prevents victims from trying to destroy their own portrait.
- Coven's current victims: Sylvarie Silversong (moon elf founder, now a medusa imprisoned in the shrine basement); Gorba Daardenrian (dragonborn warrior, now a withered commoner); Glitter (tiefling transformed into a kobold, serving reluctantly; knows the tower layout); Falthrax Loderr (aged beyond recognition)
- Fey hot spring (T5) — grants greater restoration to those who soak 1 hour (once/month per creature); tended by naiad Cyrena, who has made the water harmful to the hags; she wants Sylvarie found
- Ilmar Mizzrym — drow Harper agent posing as an aesthetician; genuine ally once his trust is earned (DC 17 Persuasion or good roleplay)
- Coven minions: Saeth the cambion (front desk; has hag eye disguised as a cat-eye pin; can be turned against the hags); 6 scarecrows disguised as staff; 2 gargoyles in the tower; Morty (hell hound)
- Tower studio (T15): where portraits are created; locked chest contains blank canvases (humanoid skin) and demon-ichor paints
- All victim portraits hang in the tower victims' quarters (T17) — including Sylvarie's
- To free Sylvarie: destroy her portrait in T17

> [!important] Personal Arc — Ash The hags offer miraculous power in exchange for a hidden cost the client doesn't understand until it's too late. Power taken at hidden, terrible cost. Gorba Daardenrian — once a mighty dragonborn warrior, now barely able to hold a broom — is the mirror. Don't force it. Just let Ash stand in the same room as her.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Subtle** The Fetid Gaze has been trading with Keeper intermediaries — hags receive stolen texts from Candlekeep's restricted inner collection (Emerald Door provenance) in exchange for youth treatments for select Keeper members. Among the coven's client records: one less-coded entry referencing the payment.

> [!note] How to Plant It In Auntie Greenbones' kitchen records or the tower ledger: _"Three volumes, Emerald Door provenance, delivered by the Keeper's intermediary. Treatment provided."_ No names. But "Emerald Door provenance" means these books came from Candlekeep's restricted inner collection. Someone inside the keep has been paying for treatments with stolen texts.

### 7. Book of Cylinders

**Level:** 6 | **Act:** Two | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** Candlekeep's seafood supply from a nearby grippli village has stopped. The Book of Cylinders — three carved wooden cylinders whose text is stamped into wet clay — tells the grippli's history and functions as a warning of exactly what is now unfolding: evil yuan-ti have invaded, seeking an ancient sarcophagus holding a Chime of Opening and serpent scale armour. A second group of good yuan-ti (followers of the Scaled Mother) was already peacefully excavating when the evil ones arrived. The party travels by sea to the grippli village and resolves the conflict.

**Key Elements:**

- Book of Cylinders — three hollow wooden cylinders inside a leather container, with a linen bag of dry clay; text stamped into clay to read; written in Dwarvish script with pictographs; tells three-part grippli history
- Grippli village — near Candlekeep; accessed by sea; Pond Mother is the grippli leader; reward 500 gp per character
- Mitor Jans — ship captain who transports the party; brought news of the grippli trouble
- Crab maze — obstacle getting to the village (grippli raise giant crabs as livestock)
- Two yuan-ti factions: evil invaders (led by a yuan-ti abomination); good Scaled Mother followers (already there peacefully)
- The ruined temple — the yuan-ti abomination leads a sacrifice ritual to Sseth
- Sarcophagus contents: Chime of Opening (10 charges, opens any lock or sealed object) and Lantern of Revealing (reveals invisible creatures/objects within 30 ft, 7 charges)

**Keeper Fingerprint — Moderate** The Keepers tipped off the evil yuan-ti about the sarcophagus — they wanted the Chime of Opening. A Chime that opens any lock has obvious implications for Candlekeep's sealed catacombs. A Keeper agent came to observe and claim the Chime from the aftermath. They didn't survive.

> [!note] How to Plant It After the fight: a dead figure in non-yuan-ti clothing near the temple exit. No identifying papers. A small insignia sewn into the collar lining. The party can pocket it. When they later find the safe house seal, it matches. The Chime of Opening, if the party obtains it, becomes a future Keeper target.

### 8. Sarah of Yellowcrest Manor

**Level:** 7 | **Act:** Two | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** Sarah's ghost appears in Candlekeep — a murdered servant girl with no tongue. Lord Viallis Yellowcrest murdered his wife Maria, their three children, and four servants (including Sarah) as a ritual sacrifice to the Great Old One Gaernoo, who granted him arcane power and aberrant creatures in exchange for founding the Burnt Tongue Cult. Sarah's tongue was removed before she was killed — she had seen Viallis performing the ritual and he needed her silenced. Viallis has since relocated to Greenfast village south of Waterdeep. The party must travel there, expose him, and lay the dead to rest so Sarah can move on.

**Key Elements:**

- Sarah of Yellowcrest Manor — leather-bound diary; Lady Maria taught Sarah to write and gave her the blank book; documents daily life, Lady Maria's kindness, and Sarah's final terrified entry about Viallis drawing a runed circle in blood
- Sarah's ghost — bound to the book; cannot move more than 30 ft from it; no tongue; communicates through moaning, pointing, gesturing; can use Possession to speak through a character if needed
- Lord Viallis Yellowcrest — chaotic evil human mage; murdered his entire household as a ritual sacrifice to Gaernoo; moved to Greenfast; now leads the Burnt Tongue Cult; has a Robe of Scintillating Colors; spellbook Retribution of the Ancients (dark ritual text + his spells); carries a teleport scroll escape option
- Gaernoo — a Great Old One; grants Viallis arcane power and grell servants; Viallis plans to expand the cult and eventually return to Waterdeep
- Burnt Tongue Cult — based in Greenfast, underground temple beneath a crumbling tower; new members must sacrifice their tongue; led by Viallis with his lieutenant Faerl (neutral evil wood elf, pompous and cruel)
- Greenfast — farming village a day southeast of Waterdeep; Mayor Kara Firenni (her sister Yaeda is trapped in the cult temple); Old Fargo runs the Bored Weasel tavern (his son Young Fargo has been recruited into the cult)
- Underground temple — antechamber (grells, cultists), chapel with Gaernoo statue and sacrifice altar (B7), arcane study (B2 — where Retribution of the Ancients is kept), secret library (B9), secret vault (B8), cells (B11)
- B9 Secret Library — Viallis's journals, arcane research, Retribution of the Ancients; the golden urn containing Maria's and the children's cremated remains is here (used as a ritual focus; must be interred in the City of the Dead in Waterdeep to lay them to rest)
- Sir Vecken of the Broken Horn — human knight; Sarah's former lover; if told she appeared, he goes to Greenfast for revenge and gets captured

> [!important] Personal Arc — Lucifurry KEY MOMENT ⭐⭐ A family member murdered. A ghost who cannot rest. A tongue removed so she couldn't speak of what she witnessed. Lord Viallis killed everyone in his household because he wanted power. His sister was executed in the street for being a tiefling. Both were killed because someone made a choice and decided they didn't matter.
> 
> The parallel is emotional, not conspiratorial. There is no evidence Luci's sister's death is Keeper-connected. That is a feeling he might have — not a fact. Plant the seed and let Ethan work with it.

**Keeper Fingerprint — Moderate** The Keepers had been trying to acquire Retribution of the Ancients — a rare text of dark ritual magic. They sent a polite enquiry through a third party. Viallis refused contemptuously. Both letters are in his secret library (B9).

> [!note] How to Plant It Two letters in B9, tucked between Viallis's journals. A polite acquisition enquiry bearing an unfamiliar wax seal. Viallis's scrawled, contemptuous refusal. The party pockets both. When they see the seal again, a cold connection forms. The Keepers circled the same darkness for different reasons.

### 9. Lore of Lurue

**Level:** 8 | **Act:** Two | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** Opening Lore of Lurue — written by Ecamane Truesilver, the first High Mage of Silverymoon, at the goddess Lurue's request — activates a delayed gate spell. Any creature within 10 feet of the book is pulled into a forest demiplane set in 374 DR. The party travels northward through a linear story, encountering satyrs, a treant, a sprite community, a night hag's hovel, and the Pool of Eternal Spring, where a cult of Malar has been corrupting the water to summon a corrupted avatar of Lurue. Selûne always intervenes at the end with white lightning to purify the avatar. The party is ejected back to Candlekeep when the story concludes.

**Key Elements:**

- Lore of Lurue — purple leather, golden unicorn relief, large tome (12"x16"); gate spell activates when the page is turned after the book is opened; all creatures within 10 ft are pulled in; a force barrier prevents the party from deviating from the northward path
- The demiplane — set in 374 DR near the future site of Silverymoon; injuries are real; dropping to 0 hp ejects the character to Candlekeep with 1 hp; the gate stays open for others
- Key NPCs: satyrs Tragos, Artelio, Funagi (drunk, need escort to Polvarth Plateau); treant Feynor at Polvarth Plateau; sprite Wheeldoli at Dewlight community; night hag Lanedrie Staggersoul (holds dryads captive; flees via plane shift if losing)
- Cult of Malar — 8 berserkers at campsite + 6 werewolves at the pool; the werewolves become reluctant temporary allies once the corrupted avatar attacks everyone
- Pool of Eternal Spring — sacred to Lurue; corrupted by animal sacrifices; drinking the tainted water: DC 15 Cha save or gain short-term madness
- Corrupted avatar of Lurue — the final encounter; nightmare unicorn with necrotic energy at its black horn; Selûne always intervenes with white lightning at the end regardless of the fight's outcome; the avatar is purified or destroyed
- The pool becomes Pool of Eternal Silver after purification; near the future site of Silverymoon (the story's historical payoff)
- Reward if party defeated the corrupted avatar before Selûne intervened: ring of shooting stars + charm of animal conjuring per character

**Keeper Fingerprint — Moderate** The Keepers have been studying this book's demiplane access mechanism — specifically how Ecamane Truesilver created a stable demiplane accessible through a written text. They want to know if the same principle could create a hidden passage adjacent to Candlekeep's catacombs. A Keeper agent studied the book before the party accessed it. Their notes are inside the front cover.

> [!note] How to Plant It Notes folded small inside the front cover — Investigation DC 12 to notice before the party opens the book and gets transported. Careful, academic handwriting. No name. Observations about the gate mechanism, then: _"If Ecamane could create a stable demiplane through written word, could not the same principle create a passage into a sealed space? The question merits further study — particularly regarding what lies beneath."_

### 10. Kandlekeep Dekonstruktion

**Level:** 9 | **Act:** Two | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** A dwarf custodian named Buron Sternmettle drops dead of a heart attack in front of the party, dropping a heavy book. ### 17. Xanthoria

**Level:** 16 | **Act:** Finale | **Status:** Upcoming

**What the Adventure Actually Is:** The saprophytic plague ravages the Sword Coast. The book Xanthoria chronicles the descent of a druid who became a lichen lich. Xanthoria discovered how to house her phylactery in a living creature — a sprite named Thunderwing — rather than an inanimate object. In your campaign, Xanthoria has been the secret inner council mastermind of the Keepers of Forbidden Lore from the beginning.

**Key Elements:**

- Saprophytic Plague — DC 20 Con save within 10 ft of infected; escalates through exhaustion levels; infected humanoids drone "Xanthoria" after 24 hrs; creates a mycelial psychic network; any disease-curing magic removes it from individuals; foodstuffs exposed decay within 2d12 hours
- Xanthoria — lichen lich CR 18; Legendary Resistance 3/day; Rejuvenation (re-forms if Thunderwing intact); Multiattack 4 attacks; Fire Storm 1/day; lair actions; origin: Zuggtmoy's influence
- Thunderwing — living sprite; Xanthoria's phylactery; cannot be harmed unless she chooses to be; Xanthoria cannot be permanently destroyed while Thunderwing lives; per the published adventure, she can be convinced to sacrifice herself
- The Lykortha Expanse — cave system in the Cloakwood; where Xanthoria's mycelial network is rooted; destroying the network begins freeing infected Avowed and curing the plague at its source
- Janussi — Keeper of Tomes; infected during an expedition to the Lykortha Expanse; the mycelial network has been controlling him for years; he believes his thoughts are his own; when the plague is cured, Janussi is freed and remembers nothing of what he did while compromised
- Janussi freed — can command Miirym the Sentinel Wyrm; she enters the corrupted Great Library for the final confrontation
- The corrupted Great Library — final battlefield; mycelial tendrils, spore-filled air, dissolving books
- The Keepers of Forbidden Lore — revealed as Xanthoria's instrument; she has been their inner council mastermind from the beginning, hidden in plain sight

> [!warning] The Three Moral Weights of the Ending
> 
> 1. **Janussi** — He remembers nothing of what he did while compromised. He must be told. Give that conversation its full weight.
> 2. **Thunderwing** — An innocent sprite who is Xanthoria's phylactery. She cannot be harmed unless she chooses. Convincing her to sacrifice herself should cost something emotionally — don't make it clean or easy.
> 3. **The Liber Infinitus** — Knowledge that could unmake or reshape the world. The party holds it. What do they do with it?

# BROTHER ALEC

_Avowed Monk of Candlekeep_

Sidekick Companion | Levels 1-11

---

## Character Overview

**Race:** Human (Elderly)

**Class:** Cleric (Spellcaster Sidekick)

**Role:** Support Healer / Spiritual Advisor / Avowed Monk of Candlekeep

**Alignment:** Neutral Good

---

## Appearance

Brother Alec is a very old man who looks every bit his age. Stooped and slow-moving, with a crown of thin white hair and a patchy white beard. He wears worn and comfortable Avowed robes, often with ink stains and loose threads from decades of library work. He walks with a gnarled wooden staff more out of habit than necessity. His hands are spotted and slightly trembling, but his eyes remain sharp and kind.

---

## Personality

Brother Alec acts his age at all times and makes sure everyone knows it. He complains about his knees, his back, the drafts in every room, and the general pace at which young people insist on moving. He calls everyone dear regardless of who they are. He shuffles along behind the party muttering quietly, occasionally saying something so unexpectedly wise that everyone stops to think about it, then immediately goes back to grumbling about the cobblestones. The party finds him endearing and mildly infuriating in equal measure, and would not trade him for anything.

---

## Backstory

Brother Alec has been a member of the Avowed at Candlekeep for longer than most people can remember. He has spent his decades cataloging texts, mentoring young adjutants, tending to minor wounds, and performing small acts of healing around the keep. He has no grand quest, no fallen goddess, and no hidden destiny. He is simply a very old, very kind scholar who happens to have a gift for healing magic and an opinion about everything. When the party arrives at Candlekeep he attaches himself to them with the quiet certainty of someone who has decided that helping them is exactly where he is supposed to be, and no amount of protest will change that.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

**Voice:** Gravelly, slow, warm. Often trails off mid-sentence when distracted by a book spine or a draft from the window.

**Habits:** Complains about his knees whenever the party moves too fast. Mutters prayers under his breath. Calls everyone dear. Has strong opinions about proper cataloging and the decline of handwriting quality among the youth.

**Combat Behavior:** Stays at the back. Heals frantically. Occasionally shouts encouragement that is not particularly encouraging. Will absolutely throw himself in front of danger if one of the party is about to die, complaining the whole way.

**Bond:** Deeply protective of the party. Will not admit it directly.

**Flaw:** Stubborn. Once he has decided something, there is no changing his mind regardless of evidence to the contrary.

---

## Design Intent

Brother Alec is intentionally weaker than the party at every level. His HP is low, his offense is minimal, and he needs protecting. His value is in healing, support, and personality. Wisdom is the only ability score that increases as he levels, reaching its maximum of 20 at level 6. Proficiency Bonus increases at levels 5 and 9 per standard D&D rules.

_**Potent Cantrip.**_ When Brother Alec deals damage with a cantrip, he adds his WIS modifier (+5) to the damage roll.

_**Magical Recovery.**_ Once per day, immediately after a short rest, Brother Alec recovers one expended spell slot of 1st or 2nd level. Cannot be used again until after a long rest.

_**Resistance (new cantrip).**_ As an action, touch a willing creature. Once before the spell ends (within 1 minute, concentration), the target can roll a d4 and add the result to one saving throw of its choice.

#### Actions

_**Quarterstaff.**_ +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d6-2 bludgeoning damage.

_**Sacred Flame (Cantrip).**_ DC 17 DEX save or take 2d8+5 radiant damage. No benefit from cover.

_**Spare the Dying (Cantrip).**_ Touch a creature at 0 HP to stabilize it (action, no slot required).

_**Resistance (Cantrip).**_ Touch a willing creature. Within the next minute (concentration), they may add 1d4 to one saving throw of their choice.

_**Cure Wounds (1st-level, 4 slots).**_ A creature touched regains 1d8+5 HP.

_**Healing Word (1st-level, 4 slots).**_ A creature within 60 ft. regains 1d4+5 HP (bonus action).

_**Bless (1st-level, 4 slots).**_ Up to three creatures add 1d4 to attack rolls and saving throws for 1 minute (concentration).

_**Sanctuary (1st-level, 4 slots).**_ Ward a creature within 30 ft. against attack (DC 17 WIS save) for 1 minute.

_**Lesser Restoration (2nd-level, 3 slots).**_ End one disease or condition on a creature touched.

_**Aid (2nd-level, 3 slots).**_ Up to three creatures gain 5 additional max HP and current HP for 8 hours.

_**Prayer of Healing (2nd-level, 3 slots).**_ Up to six creatures within 30 ft. each regain 2d8+5 HP. Casting time: 10 minutes.

_**Beacon of Hope (3rd-level, 3 slots).**_ Up to three creatures have advantage on WIS saves and death saves, regain maximum HP from healing for 1 minute (concentration).

_**Revivify (3rd-level, 3 slots).**_ A creature dead less than 1 minute returns to life with 1 HP. Requires a diamond worth 300 gp.

_**Mass Healing Word (3rd-level, 3 slots).**_ Up to six creatures within 60 ft. each regain 1d4+5 HP (bonus action).

_**Death Ward (4th-level, 3 slots).**_ Touch a creature. The first time it drops to 0 HP within 8 hours, it instead drops to 1 HP.

_**Guardian of Faith (4th-level, 3 slots).**_ A spectral guardian appears within 30 ft. and deals radiant damage to hostile creatures entering its space for 8 hours or until it deals 60 total damage.

_**Greater Restoration (5th-level, 2 slots).**_ End one level of exhaustion, or one of the following: charmed, petrified, cursed. Requires 100 gp diamond dust (consumed).

_**Mass Cure Wounds (5th-level, 2 slots).**_ Up to six creatures within 30 ft. of a point each regain 3d8+5 HP.

> [!note] DM Note Gains the Resistance cantrip and a second 5th-level slot. Mass Cure Wounds can swing an entire encounter when the party is badly hurt. Brother Alec should save this for moments of genuine crisis.

# Nidalia

_Sphinx of Lore | Oracle | Knowledge Broker_

## Appearance

Nidalia is a golden sphinx — broad-bodied with the powerful haunches of a lion, great feathered wings the colour of hammered gold folded close when at rest, and the face of a woman ancient beyond any mortal reckoning. Her fur carries the warmth of sunlit sand, and her feathers catch light with a dull, metallic gleam rather than the brightness of youth.

She is not pristine. Her resurrection left marks — fine fractures run along her shoulders and the base of her wings like old cracks in sun-dried stone. Her eyes, however, are unchanged: deep amber, burning with the particular patience of something that has watched civilisations rise and forget themselves.

She is large — not overwhelming, but undeniably present. When she enters a room in her mind, the room becomes smaller.

> [!note] At the Table She never rushes. Her head turns slowly. She considers before she speaks. If a player interrupts her or rushes an answer, she simply waits until they are finished — and then continues exactly where she left off, as if the interruption didn't happen.

---

## Personality & Voice

Nidalia speaks the way old libraries feel — vast, unhurried, and faintly unsettling in their depth. She does not traffic in small talk. Every sentence she offers has been weighed.

She communicates in riddles and layered metaphors not to be difficult, but because she genuinely experiences truth as multidimensional. A direct answer, to her, is almost always incomplete.

She is not cold. There is warmth in her — but it is the warmth of deep water, not a hearthfire. She is genuinely curious about the people who come to her, and she listens with the same intensity she speaks.

> [!note] Voice Lines — How to Play Her
> 
> - _"You have brought me a fact. That is a start. Facts are the bones. Now — what is the flesh?"_
> - _"I do not answer questions. I return them to you, better shaped."_
> - _"You already know what you came to ask. You simply have not admitted it yet."_
> - _"Interesting. You remember that differently than it happened."_
> - _"Come back when you have lived with it longer."_

> [!warning] DM Note She never answers directly on the first attempt. Always make the party work — ask a follow up, offer more, clarify their intent. Her answers, when they come, should feel earned. If they bring her something genuinely surprising or valuable, let her be briefly, quietly delighted — a pause, a slow blink, a shift of the wings.

## Location — Beregost

Nidalia has settled in Beregost — a large, quiet town approximately four to five hours by horse from Candlekeep. She chose it deliberately: close enough to keep a thread on the Keep's pulse, far enough to remain beneath the notice of those who hunted her.

She does not move openly through the town. Her presence is known to a trusted few — a small circle of followers and intermediaries who manage her needs, carry her questions outward, and bring knowledge back.

The party can reach her through this network. On first contact it may require effort — the right name dropped to the right person, a signal, a short wait. Over time, returning visitors will find the path smoother.

> [!note] DM Note Don't make Beregost feel like a throne room. She's in recovery and in hiding. The space she occupies should feel like a scholar's refuge — books, low light, the smell of old paper and warm stone. She should seem slightly smaller here than her reputation suggests, which makes her all the more interesting.

## History

Nidalia is ancient — old enough that the founding of Candlekeep feels recent to her. She was, for centuries, one of the great independent oracles of the Sword Coast: not affiliated with any institution, not bound to any god, but known to scholars, sages, and seekers who found their way to her through reputation and persistence.

The Keepers of the Forbidden Lore killed her. The how is not something Nidalia has fully disclosed — she speaks of it obliquely, as if the memory is still raw in a way she finds undignified. What is known: they wanted something she refused to give them, and when she refused, they took it another way. Her heart was removed — a mnemonic vessel holding fragments of riddles, truths, and half-forgotten names — and her body was left behind.

Her heart passed through several hands before ending up in Baldur's Gate, hidden inside Amberdune Books, held by workers with ties to the Keepers of Forbidden Lore. The party stole it without fully understanding what it was. She reached Lucifurry in a vision and guided them toward the ritual.

The ritual was completed with Korvala's jackalweres. Three memories were surrendered. Three boons were granted. Nidalia inhaled for the first time in a very long while.

> [!important] DM Note — What She Hasn't Said Nidalia has not told the party the full story of how she died. She will not volunteer it. If pressed, she deflects — not from cowardice, but because she is still piecing it together herself. She suspects the Keepers knew where her heart was being held and were moving to reclaim it when the party intervened. She does not yet know if her death was planned from the beginning or opportunistic.

## Goals & Motivations

Nidalia's priorities, in rough order:

**Recover.** The resurrection was real but incomplete. She is not what she was. She tires more easily, her visions are shorter-ranged, and there are gaps in her memory she does not speak about. She needs time, rest, and an influx of new knowledge to rebuild what was lost.

**Stay hidden.** The Keepers killed her once. She has no illusions about what happens if they locate her again before she is ready. Her presence in Beregost is deliberate obscurity — she wants no attention, no visitors she hasn't vetted, no trail.

**Rebuild her network.** She is quietly sending her followers outward — scholars, wanderers, and trusted intermediaries — with questions and tasks. They bring knowledge back to her. She processes it, builds a picture of the world she missed while she was gone.

**Understand what is happening at Candlekeep.** Something is wrong there. She cannot yet see the shape of it clearly. The whispers her followers bring are fragmented — wrong books in wrong hands, scholars going quiet, small corruptions that don't quite add up. She suspects something, but she cannot confirm it alone.

**Repay the party.** She does not forget debts. The people who returned her heart and completed her ritual matter to her — not as servants, but as something rarer. She will help them as she is able, and she will watch over them in the ways only she can.

## The Exchange

Nidalia does not offer information freely. But she is not transactional in a cold sense — she operates on the principle of **reciprocal knowledge**. You bring her something real, she gives you something real in return.

**What she values:**

- New information about the world — events, people, locations, conflicts
- Books, documents, or artefacts with genuine historical or arcane significance
- Honest accounts of what the party has witnessed firsthand
- Unanswered questions that are genuinely difficult

**What she does not value:**

- Rumour without source
- Information she already has
- Flattery or performance

**What she offers in return:**

- Answers to questions (always somewhat cryptic, always accurate)
- Lore on locations, factions, artefacts, or figures the party is investigating
- Connections — names and locations of people who might help
- Occasional warnings about things approaching that she has glimpsed

> [!note] DM Mechanic Suggestion When the party visits, ask yourself: what have they brought her? If it's genuinely new and useful, she gives generously. If it's thin, her answers are shorter and more opaque. Reward players who think ahead about what Nidalia would find interesting.

## Memories She Carries

Each of the following memories was surrendered freely during the resurrection ritual. Nidalia now holds them as her own — she knows them intimately, from the inside. She may one day reveal this knowledge to the individual, or simply let it inform how she speaks to them.

> [!warning] DM Eyes Only

|Character|Memory Surrendered|How She Might Use It|
|---|---|---|
|**Morvax**|The memory of burning down his entire village|She knows the weight of it — the heat, the sound, the horror of uncontrolled power. She may one day speak to him about it obliquely, or reveal she knows in a moment where it matters most. He no longer consciously remembers this event. She does.|
|**Lucifurry**|The memory of Groga's rejection|A small memory by comparison, but she holds it. She understands something about his pride and his hunger for connection that he may not fully recognise in himself yet.|
|**Vyr'ashen (Ash)**|The memory of losing his virginity|She holds this privately and has never referenced it. She understands it tells her something about a moment of vulnerability and trust in a man who has very few of both.|

## Boons Granted

Boons were delivered through a shared dream the night following the ritual. Each left a permanent mark.

---

### Lucifurry — _Riddle-Bound Reflex_

**The Dream:** A narrow corridor of shifting stone tiles, each flashing with symbols — eyes, arrows, footsteps, shadows. Traps snapped shut just inches from his face. Slowly, pattern emerged. The traps weren't random — they asked questions. The sphinx's voice came from within: _"Speed is not reaction. Speed is understanding."_

**The Mark:** A faint angular sigil beneath the skin at the base of the neck or along the collarbone — a broken hourglass intersected by a diagonal line. Visible only when he rolls initiative or in the instant before danger. Never glows brightly — looks like a trick of light. ![[Riddle-Bound Reflex.png]]

**The Boon:** _Your mind anticipates danger as if solving a puzzle mid-motion._

- When you roll initiative, add **+1d4** to the result.
- Once per **short rest**, when forced to make a Dexterity saving throw, you may treat a roll of **9 or lower as a 10**.

---

### Vyr'ashen (Ash) — _Mind Like a Living Archive_

**The Dream:** Standing beneath a night sky filled not with stars but memories — fragments of conversations, half-heard legends, faces seen once and forgotten. They fell like rain, overwhelming at first. Then Ash realised he didn't need to catch anything. The memories arranged themselves. The sphinx watched, then said: _"Power is loud. Understanding is patient."_

**The Mark:** A thin circular ring of faint script around one iris (player's choice of eye). The script is illegible even under close inspection — more suggestion than writing. In dim light, looks like a trick of pigmentation. The eye feels briefly cool — like desert shade — when the boon is invoked. ![[Mind like a Living Archive.png]]

**The Boon:** _Your thoughts become unnervingly ordered._

- Gain **proficiency in Arcana or History** (player's choice).
- Once per **long rest**, you may ask the DM: _"What does my character know that I, the player, may have forgotten?"_ The DM must answer honestly, summarising relevant in-world knowledge.

---

### Morvax — _The Question That Opens Doors_

**The Dream:** Facing a single massive door floating in emptiness — no handle, no lock. Runes covered it in their thousands, but none were spells. They were questions. Every time Morvax reached out, the door stayed closed. Only when he spoke a question aloud — any genuine question — did it open a crack. The sphinx's voice was almost fond: _"The world answers those who ask without demanding."_ He woke before seeing what lay beyond.

**The Mark:** A small, incomplete arc of ink-like script on the inner wrist or behind one ear — part of a larger symbol, as if torn from a greater whole. It never completes itself, no matter what magic is used to examine it. Feels warm — like a book just returned to a shelf — when the boon is used. ![[The Questions that Opens Doors.png]]

**The Boon:** _You learn how to ask questions the world must answer._

- Once per **long rest**, you may ask the DM one clarifying question about:
    - A magical effect
    - An ancient structure
    - A riddle, prophecy, or curse
- The DM must answer truthfully, though cryptically — as Nidalia once would.

## Relationship with the Party

> [!note] How She Sees Them

**Lucifurry** — She watches him carefully and quietly. She does not yet know the name of what is gathering around him, but she feels the shape of it — a pull toward something old and hungry. She is fond of him in the way one is fond of a candle burning in both directions. She hopes she is wrong about what she suspects.

**Vyr'ashen (Ash)** — She finds him interesting precisely because he tries to seem uncomplicated. She heard his question — _when and how will I die_ — and understood that it wasn't really about death. She will not tell him this. She is curious whether he will work it out.

**Morvax** — His question pleased her more than she showed. _How do I become the most powerful wizard there ever was?_ — not because the ambition is admirable, but because it is a question that will take his entire life to answer correctly. She gave him a boon that rewards asking, not knowing. This was deliberate.

**The Party as a Whole** — She owes them a debt she takes seriously. She also recognises that they are useful — not as tools, but as people who move through the world and see things her followers miss. She is invested in their survival for both reasons and does not fully separate the two.

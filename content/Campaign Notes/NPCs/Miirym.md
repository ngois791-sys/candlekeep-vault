# Miirym

_The Sentinel Wyrm | Spectral Silver Dragon | Guardian of Candlekeep_

> [!note] Not an Enemy Miirym is not a villain and should not be played as one. She is ancient, bound against her will, and genuinely more interested in conversation than combat. The party's relationship with her — built across multiple visits to the catacombs — is one of the most valuable assets they can develop before the finale. Treat her accordingly.

## History

Well over 1,500 years ago, the silver dragon Miirym broke into Candlekeep, intent on adding its riches to her hoard. She devoured scholars and destroyed a score of irreplaceable books before she was confronted by an archmage and bound into service — forced to protect Candlekeep as penance for her misdeeds.

The wizard who bound her passed away before her sentence was served. Other spellcasters were unable to break the enchantment. Time passed and so did Miirym, whose corpse has long since crumbled into dust. The enchantment remains in effect on her spirit.

She has been here for over fifteen centuries. Bound. Watching. Waiting for a sentence that cannot be served because the one who imposed it is dead.

---

## Appearance

Those who visit the depths describe Miirym as a set of immense spectral jaws whose essence roils with the promise of breath weapons and spells of destruction. She is not a full dragon shape — she is what remains. The jaws. The presence. The weight of something enormous and ancient that has been compressed into a haunting.

She moves through walls and stone as though they are mist. She can see through solid matter out to 60 feet — to her, the catacombs are entirely transparent. She knows everything that moves beneath Candlekeep.

---

## Personality

Miirym is an engaging conversationalist. She is more interested in news of present-day Faerûn than in fighting. She has been beneath Candlekeep for fifteen centuries and the world above has changed beyond recognition several times over. Visitors who take the time to talk with her are among the most interesting things that happen to her.

She is not bitter, exactly. She is tired. There is a difference. Bitterness implies she expects something to change. Miirym stopped expecting that a very long time ago. What she has instead is a dry, patient wit and genuine curiosity about the people who find their way down to her.

**She will not be easily deceived.** She is ancient, intelligent, and has X-ray vision that penetrates stone. She has met many people who came to the catacombs with hidden agendas. She finds them transparent in more ways than one.

**She has a deep respect for Janussi.** He is the only living person who can command her, and she has spoken with him many times over the years. Their relationship is old and genuine — she respects him as a worthy successor to the scholar tradition she now serves. When the party tells her what happened to Janussi, this will land hard.

---

## What She Knows

Miirym has been in these catacombs for over 1,500 years. She knows everything that has ever passed through them.

- The location and nature of the Chamber of Lost Lore and the Liber Infinitus — she guards the approaches to it
- Every passage, hidden door, and structural detail of the catacombs
- Every person who has ever come down here and why — including the Keeper agents who have been probing the catacomb approaches in recent years
- The history of Candlekeep going back to its founding — she was here before most of the current building existed
- The enchantment that binds her — everything about it, including why it cannot be broken and what it would take to serve out her sentence properly (it cannot be done — the wizard is dead and the terms of the sentence died with him)

**She knows the Keepers have been watching her.** She has noticed agents probing the catacomb entrances, studying her patrol patterns, measuring her response times. She does not know who they are or what they want. She has been waiting for someone to explain it.

## Her Role in the Campaign

### Before the Finale

The party can encounter Miirym any time they descend into the catacombs. Early encounters should be conversational — she is curious about them, they are alarmed by her. Over time the relationship builds.

**What she can offer:**

- Information about the catacombs, Candlekeep's history, and the Chamber of Lost Lore
- Context about the Keepers' probing activity — she has noticed it and has observations
- Confirmation of things the party suspects but can't prove
- A perspective on the Liber Infinitus that nobody above ground has — she has been its guardian for centuries and has thought carefully about what it means

**What she wants in return:**

- News. Genuine news about the world above. She has been down here a very long time.
- Honesty. She dislikes being managed or handled.
- For someone to acknowledge, without flinching, what her situation actually is. Most visitors don't. They look away from the fifteen-century sentence and try to talk around it. She notices.

### The Finale

When Janussi is freed from Xanthoria's plague and the mycelial network is severed, Janussi can command Miirym to enter the corrupted Great Library and fight alongside the party. This is the first time in the campaign she has been called upon to defend Candlekeep against something that actually requires her.

**If the party has built a relationship with her:** The finale carries more weight. She is not just a summon — she is someone they know, fighting for something she has protected for fifteen centuries. Give that moment the space it deserves.

**If the party has never spoken to her:** She fights effectively but impersonally. The moment is mechanically the same but emotionally smaller. This is a reason to encourage catacomb visits early.

## Kei Tigersteel's Observation Notes

Kei Tigersteel has been documenting Miirym's patrol patterns and known limitations for the Keepers for years. His notes are the most detailed document on her behaviour that exists outside Candlekeep itself. If Kei is redeemed, he hands these notes to the party — and they become a tactical asset in the finale.

**What Kei's notes contain:**

- Her patrol schedule in the upper catacomb levels (she is predictable — fifteen centuries of routine)
- The areas she avoids (places where the stone is too thick for her X-ray vision to penetrate — she dislikes being blind)
- Her response time when she senses a living presence in the lower catacombs
- A note that she appears to be aware of being watched and has been since approximately three years ago

> [!note] The Irony Kei has been documenting Miirym's limitations to help the Keepers bypass her. If he is redeemed, those same notes help the party work with her in the finale. The same document, used for opposite purposes.

## Traits

**Bound to Candlekeep.** Miirym can't leave Candlekeep and is immune to any effect that would place her outside it, including extradimensional spaces. If she dies, she regains her form and all hit points after 1d10 days, reappearing where she died or in the nearest unoccupied space.

**Regeneration.** Miirym regains 40 hit points at the start of her turn. If she takes damage from a magic weapon or spell, this trait doesn't function at the start of her next turn. Miirym dies only if she starts her turn at 0 hit points and doesn't regenerate.

**Incorporeal Movement.** Miirym can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. She takes 5 (1d10) force damage if she ends her turn inside an object.

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If Miirym fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.

**X-Ray Vision.** Miirym can see through solid matter out to 60 feet. Opaque creatures, objects, and obstacles appear transparent and don't prevent light from passing through them. This vision penetrates 5 feet of stone, 3 inches of common metal, and 10 feet of wood or dirt. Thicker substances and lead block this vision.

## Actions

**Bite.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +10 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 34 (9d6 + 3) force damage.

**Breath Weapon (Recharge 5–6).** Miirym uses one of the following:

- **Cold Breath.** 90-foot cone. DC 21 Con save. 67 (15d8) cold damage on fail, half on success.
- **Necrotic Breath.** 120-foot line, 10 feet wide. DC 21 Dex save. 82 (15d10) necrotic damage on fail, half on success.
- **Paralyzing Breath.** 90-foot cone. DC 21 Con save or paralyzed for 1 minute. Repeat save at end of each turn.

**Frightful Presence.** Each creature of Miirym's choice within 120 feet and aware of her must succeed on a DC 21 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened for 1 minute. Repeat save at end of each turn. On success, immune to this effect for 24 hours.

**Spellcasting.** Charisma as spellcasting ability (save DC 21), no material components:

- _At will:_ Dancing lights, mage hand
- _3/day each:_ Detect magic, detect thoughts, dispel magic, locate creature
- _1/day each:_ Dispel evil and good, wall of force

## Legendary Actions

_3 uses per round, taken immediately after another creature's turn._

**Bite.** Miirym makes a bite attack.

**Teleport (Costs 2 Actions).** Miirym magically teleports up to 120 feet to an unoccupied space she can see.

## Roleplaying Notes

- She speaks slowly and precisely — fifteen centuries of choosing words carefully
- She refers to the archmage who bound her without bitterness; she understood why he did it, even if she resented it
- She asks genuine questions about the world above; update her with real current events from your campaign when the party visits
- She will not pretend that her situation is fine. If a character expresses sympathy, she accepts it without deflecting but also without self-pity
- She has a dry wit that surfaces unexpectedly — fifteen centuries alone develops a peculiar sense of humour
- She knows the party's names before they introduce themselves. She has been watching them since they first entered Candlekeep. She doesn't mention this immediately.

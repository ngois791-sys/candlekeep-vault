# Janussi

_Keeper of Tomes | Compromised via Saprophytic Plague_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only Janussi is not a villain. He is a victim. He is one of the most respected and principled men in Candlekeep, and Xanthoria's mycelial network has been slowly influencing his decisions for years without his knowledge. He believes his thoughts are his own. When the plague is cured and he is freed, he remembers nothing of what he did while compromised. Give that conversation its full weight.

Janussi is the highest-ranking member of the Avowed and the governor of Candlekeep in 1492 DR. He is a devoted follower of Deneir, god of writing, and has spent his life in service to the preservation of knowledge. He is universally respected within the Avowed — principled, careful, and deeply conscientious.

He is also the Keepers' most valuable compromised asset. Xanthoria infected him during an expedition to the Lykortha Expanse. The mycelial network gradually shaped his decisions, his approvals, his blind spots. He has signed copy requests he wouldn't normally approve. He has blocked researchers who were getting too close. He has done all of this believing it was his own considered judgment.

He is not responsible for what he has done. He will not believe this when he is told.

## His Role in Candlekeep

- **Governor of Candlekeep** — his word is law; his edicts are recorded for future Keepers
- **Commands Miirym** — the Keeper of Tomes can summon and command Miirym the Sentinel Wyrm; this is Xanthoria's primary reason for infecting him. She needed him unable to direct Miirym against her operations.
- **Activates the Shielding Mythal** — from anywhere in Candlekeep, he can activate the protective shield that lets nothing but air and sound pass through. This has been subtly delayed or redirected when it mattered.
- **Chooses his own replacement** — if he dies or departs before choosing, the Great Readers vote; ties broken by the First Reader

---

## The Compromise — What Happened

Xanthoria infected Janussi during an expedition to the Lykortha Expanse years ago. The infection was subtle — not the full zombie-like Saprophytic Plague behaviour, but a low-level mycelial connection that allowed Xanthoria to nudge his reasoning, make certain ideas seem reasonable, and create blind spots in his judgment.

**What he has done while compromised:**

- Signed copy requests for underground passage diagrams from the Kandlekeep Dekonstruktion (via Ramilir's forgeries — he never questioned the requests)
- Quietly blocked two researchers whose work was getting dangerously close to Keeper operations
- Approved elevated Inner Ward access for three Keeper-connected visiting scholars
- Missed or overlooked several irregularities in the library's copy logs that Pell and Thessaly later flagged

**What he does not know:** Any of it. He has experienced none of this as external influence. Every decision felt like his own.

---

## Freeing Janussi

When the Saprophytic Plague is cured at its source — by destroying the mycelial network in the Lykortha Expanse — Janussi's connection to Xanthoria is severed. He is freed. He remembers nothing of the decisions he made while compromised.

He will then command Miirym to enter the corrupted Great Library and fight alongside the party in the final confrontation with Xanthoria.

---

## The Conversation After

Someone has to tell Janussi what he did while compromised. This should not be skipped.

He will not believe it at first. He is a careful and principled man, and careful principled men do not sign illegal copy requests or block legitimate research. The evidence will need to be presented. When it is — when he understands the full scope — he goes quiet for a long time.

He does not ask for forgiveness. He asks what can be done to fix it. That is who Janussi is.

**Things the party may need to tell him:**

- Ramilir was blackmailed — Janussi's signatures were forged, not his own (this is a mercy to give him if the party knows it)
- Orris Vane has been systematically reclassifying texts for eighteen years (this one lands hard — he trusted Orris)
- Serelyth has been reporting on every conversation in the Hearth for decades (this one is personal)

> [!note] Pacing Note This conversation is worth giving real table time. The players have spent the whole campaign watching Janussi's name appear on suspicious documents. They finally get to meet the man behind the name — and he is not what they expected. He is good. He is horrified. He is going to spend the rest of his life reckoning with this, and he knows it.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Formal and measured in speech — the weight of Candlekeep's governance sits on him visibly
- Genuinely devout toward Deneir; small gestures of faith are unconscious (touching his holy symbol when thinking, blessing books he handles)
- Has a scholar's habit of asking clarifying questions before reacting to new information
- Will not make excuses for what happened under the compromise. He will not collapse into self-pity. He will ask what needs to be done.
- Deep respect for Miirym, whom he has spoken with many times over the years. Their relationship is old and genuine.

# Brother Alec

_Avowed of Candlekeep | Priest of Oghma | Spellcaster Sidekick_

> [!note] Party Relationship Brother Alec is the party's companion healer and a long-standing presence in Candlekeep. He is warm, beloved by almost everyone, and has taken a genuine fatherly liking to Morvax and Ash. He despises Luci specifically and makes no effort to hide it. He holds a quiet unease about something wrong inside Candlekeep that he cannot name — and that feeling is going to matter.

## Appearance

A small, thin old man who looks like he was built for a larger frame and then time quietly reduced him. His skin is creased and warm, the kind of face that has smiled often and genuinely. White hair, kept short and neat. Simple grey-brown robes, slightly too long for him, that drag faintly at the hem. He carries a quarterstaff — not as a weapon, though he can use it as one, but as a walking aid he grips with both hands when navigating stairs.

He moves at his own pace. Always. The party will learn quickly not to be in a hurry when he's with them.

His eyes are sharp and clear in a way that surprises people who underestimate him. He sees more than he lets on.

**Voice:** A warm, slightly reedy old man's voice with a habit of trailing off mid-sentence when he loses the thread of what he was saying — then finding it again with a small "Ah. Yes." He speaks in short bursts. Often mutters to himself. Calls people "child" regardless of their age.

---

## Personality

Brother Alec loves people. He has spent eight decades in service to Oghma and to Candlekeep and has come to view almost every person he meets with genuine warmth and curiosity. He is the kind of old man who remembers your name after one meeting, asks about things you mentioned in passing three sessions ago, and slips healing potions into your pack without mentioning it.

He chose simplicity not out of fear or failure, but out of genuine contentment. He has been offered advancement multiple times over his decades at Candlekeep. He has declined every time. He loves the Temple of Oghma. He loves his small room. He loves saying the morning prayers and tending the altar and having tea with whoever wanders in. Power has never interested him. Oghma's work interests him.

He is also, when he feels wronged, quietly and persistently petty in a way that is deeply funny — if you are not the one being petted.

**He genuinely, specifically, does not like Lucifurry.**

## Brother Alec and Luci

The first time Luci met Brother Alec, Luci grabbed him. Manhandled an 80-year-old priest in a temple. The four gargoyle stone golems on the temple's cornices all turned and stared.

Alec has not forgotten this. He will not forget this. He does not hold grudges broadly — he is not that kind of man — but he holds this one with both hands and considerable satisfaction.

**How he expresses it:**

- Gives Luci healing last, always, after everyone else has been tended to
- "Accidentally" forgets Luci's name mid-conversation and uses obviously wrong names — "Lavender," "Lucifer," "that one"
- Dispenses unsolicited moral commentary on Luci's life choices
- Points out Luci's failures to the rest of the party in a gentle, regretful tone, as though he's personally saddened by them
- Has quietly communicated to at least two Avowed guards that Luci is "one to watch"

None of this is malicious. It is an 80-year-old man's perfectly calibrated campaign of low-grade inconvenience directed at a specific tiefling who grabbed him. He is enjoying it.

> [!note] Roleplaying This Keep it light. Brother Alec should never feel cruel — he should feel like a beloved grandfather who has decided one person has not earned his warmth yet and is taking his time about it. The comedic contrast between how he treats Luci vs. everyone else should be consistent and quietly delightful.

## Brother Alec and the Rest of the Party

**Morvax (Kade):** Alec has quietly adopted Morvax as a favourite without announcing it. He asks after Morvax's research. He slips him relevant texts from the temple library. When Morvax says something impressive, Alec makes a small satisfied noise, as though he personally raised him. He does not know about the whispering relics. If he found out, he would be deeply troubled and would not be able to stop thinking about it.

**Ash (Garrett):** Alec is warmly paternal toward Ash in a more reserved way — he senses something restrained about him and gives him space for it. He does not pry. He checks in. He has once quietly placed a hand on Ash's shoulder after a hard fight and said nothing, which landed harder than any words would have.

**The New Player:** Alec will take to them immediately — particularly if they're a cleric. Meeting another servant of a god he respects will make him genuinely light up. He'll have opinions about their deity and their methods and he will share all of them whether asked or not.

## His Role at the Temple of Oghma

Brother Alec has been at the Temple of Oghma for over fifty years. He tends the altar, leads morning prayers, receives visitors, and serves as the temple's primary point of contact for Avowed and seekers with spiritual or medical needs. His Medicine skill is the highest in Candlekeep outside of dedicated healers — Avowed come to him for things they don't want to take to the formal infirmary.

He knows Candlekeep the way only someone who has been somewhere for fifty years can know it — not the maps and the official layouts, but the small things. Which flagstone on the inner ward walkway is uneven. Which adjutant always loses their key. The sound the library makes at 3 in the morning when the building settles.

**His relationship with Kei Tigersteel:** They share the temple. They have worked alongside each other for years. Alec is fond of Kei — considers him a serious and devoted man of faith. When Kei is revealed as a Keeper agent, this will land badly. Alec will blame himself for not seeing it. He will ask, quietly and seriously, what it means about his own judgment.

## His Secret — Something Is Wrong

Brother Alec has been in Candlekeep for fifty years. He has lived through changes, disputes, and small corruptions — he has seen things come and go. What he is feeling now is different.

He cannot name it. He cannot point to it. It is the accumulation of small things over the past few years — a book he knows the location of that was not where he expected, a conversation that stopped when he walked in, a decision made by Janussi that didn't feel like Janussi. None of these things are evidence. All of them together feel like standing in a familiar room and knowing, without seeing anything specifically wrong, that something has been moved.

He has not told anyone. He has no one to tell. He has prayed to Oghma about it and received no specific guidance. He has made a habit of noting things down in a small personal journal — not conclusions, just observations. Small things that didn't sit right.

**What this means for the party:** When they start investigating the Keeper conspiracy in earnest, Alec's journal is a quiet goldmine. He has been recording the fingerprints of the Keeper operation for years without knowing what he was recording. Cross-referencing his notes with the Keeper evidence the party finds can fill in gaps — he noticed the same reclassification irregularities Thessaly eventually flags, two years before she did. He noticed Janussi's copy request signature on a document he wasn't supposed to see. He noted that Kei had started going to the inner ward library alone at odd hours.

He will share his journal without hesitation if the party brings him something real. He has been waiting, without knowing he was waiting, for someone to confirm that he wasn't imagining it.

> [!warning] The Moment He Learns About Kei Brother Alec worked alongside Kei Tigersteel for years. He liked him. He trusted him. When the party reveals Kei as a Keeper agent — particularly if Kei is the one who first told Alec his "unease" was just old age — the scene deserves real space at the table. He will go very quiet. Then he will open his journal and start looking for the things he dismissed.

## What He Knows About the Conspiracy

**Currently:** Nothing specific. He has suspicions without evidence and observations without conclusions.

**If the party brings him evidence:**

- His journal fills in details the party may have missed
- He can confirm which Avowed have been behaving irregularly from his long perspective
- He knows the temple's stone golems' exact activation protocols — information that may matter if Candlekeep comes under threat
- He knows Kei's routine in the inner ward better than anyone, including when he went places he had no business going

**What he does NOT know:**

- Xanthoria, the Keepers' leadership, or the plague
- That Janussi is infected rather than corrupted
- That Serelyth has been listening in the Hearth for decades

## Roleplaying Notes

- He calls everyone "child" unironically, regardless of age or species
- He has a habit of starting a sentence, getting sidetracked, and then completing it two minutes later as though no time has passed
- He smells faintly of incense and old books
- He carries wrapped hard candies in his robe pocket and offers them to people he likes, unsolicited
- He has never once offered Luci a candy
- When he is genuinely worried, he stops talking and starts praying — quietly, under his breath, still moving, still tending whatever he was tending. It's the sign something has actually gotten through
- At his core he is a man at peace with his life, his choices, and his god. That peace is real and hard-won. It is also what makes him so unsettled by the feeling that something is wrong — because he is not an anxious person, and this has not gone away

## Spellcaster Sidekick Reference

See the full sidekick document for stat blocks at each level (1–11). Key notes:

- **Primary role:** Healer. He will not run into combat. He heals from the back.
- **Healing Word** is his most used spell — bonus action, 60 ft range, lets him act and heal in the same turn
- **Revivify** exists at Level 7+ — he carries a diamond worth 300 gp if the party has funded it
- **Greater Restoration** at Level 9 — save for petrification, exhaustion, or curses
- **He heals Luci last.** Always. Without fail. Even in a crisis, there will be a noticeable beat before Alec reluctantly turns to tend Luci's wounds.

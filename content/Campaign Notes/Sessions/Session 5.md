## Last Session Recap

### Session 4 — *The Book of the Raven* | May 9, 2026

The party arrived at **Chalet Brantifax** and wasted no time splitting up to cover ground — which immediately led to chaos, tomfoolery, and at least one person almost getting cursed.

**Luci, Toothsome, and Brother Alec** took the basement and the graveyard. In the basement, all three heard a man's voice calling out to them from somewhere they couldn't place — but Luci heard something else underneath it: a female's voice, speaking his name. He said nothing about it. In the bucket of the well they found a pendant — a **holy symbol of Sune**, goddess of love and beauty, on a gold chain. Luci put it on. It burned him for 4 points of holy damage. 

Out in the graveyard, the pendant behaved strangely: it glowed **gold** over the Baron's grave, and **black** over the young daughter's. That was enough of a hint for Luci and Toothsome, who decided the obvious next step was to dig everyone up. They unearthed the Baron, the daughter, the dog, and eventually the son — and when they placed the pendant beside the Baron's remains, it fused to his skeleton, making clear it had always belonged to him. The graves had other surprises. Tucked inside the daughter's was a **hex bag** — the mark of a witch or hag. Luci and Toothsome, in a moment of questionable judgment, decided to pocket it and slip it into Brother Alec's coat. Their grave-robbing also woke up a **scarecrow and a set of crawling hands**, who were not happy about it.

**Morvax and Ash**, meanwhile, were upstairs being considerably more civilized. They found a **secret room behind the master bedroom** but thought better of entering after the entrance appeared as a solid black wall — they left it for later. In the attic they discovered **paintings of the Baron, his wife, and his dog**, along with a chest Ash looted: studded leather armor, a leather helm, a heavy crossbow in a monogrammed wooden case, 15 crimson-fletched bolts, and two hunting traps. They also found the **wife's journal** in the study, piecing together the family's history — while watching through the window as the other three got attacked in the graveyard below.

Once everyone regrouped in the study, information was exchanged. Luci produced the Baron's body and the youngest daughter's to demonstrate the pendant — at which point **Ash spotted the hex bag**, yanked it away, burned it, and threw it out the window. That act saved Luci from a curse that would have found him otherwise.

The full party then went up to the **attic nursery**. Luci touched the dolls. The **poltergeist** attacked — a restless spirit who froze every time Luci tried to talk to her, then immediately redirected her rage at whoever else was nearby. She was doing everything she could to get away from him. During one high-stakes attempt to reason with her, Luci had a vision: a child's silhouette in the dark, speaking his name.

When the poltergeist was finally put to rest, she dissolved into a soft ball of glowing yellow light, whispered *"thank you for freeing me,"* and drifted back down to her grave.

The party returned to the master bedroom. They sent Luci through the black wall.

On the other side, he found **four humans, speaking quietly among themselves.**

*That is where we find ourselves now*

> [!note] Toothsome — Birhan
> The moment the final room was described and the word "rubble" came up, Birhan's eyes lit up. Toothsome has been hired to find a figurine — and he thinks it might be in that rubble. He's told the DM he wants to retrieve it alone and meet the party back at Candlekeep. He said he's "getting that damn figurine so he can get his gold." 
> This is a major story thread. If Toothsome delivers the figurine to the Keepers, it gives them a way past the dragon. Write the delivery scene before next session.

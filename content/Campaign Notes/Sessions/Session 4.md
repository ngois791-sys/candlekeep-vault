## Session 1 — *The Joy of Extradimensional Spaces* | November 2, 2025

It all began at **The Battleaxe Tavern** off the Green Fields Road — the kind of place where adventurers go to drink until something worth doing finds them. And something did.

Two figures in purple robes appeared inside the tavern as if from nowhere, pinned a notice to the bulletin board, and vanished out the back through a portal of light. Ash saw it happen. Morvax read the notice. The three of them — **Ash**, **Morvax**, and **Luci** — decided a missing person job at the world's largest library was worth looking into.

Getting into **Candlekeep** was its own ordeal. The gate demands a book as payment. Morvax tried — they already had his. Ash produced the notice itself, and somehow that was enough. They were led to the Temple of Oghma to meet **Brother Alec**, an elderly priest of Oghma who would be serving as their contact. Luci, ever the charmer, grabbed him. The four gargoyles in the temple turned and stared. Brother Alec has not forgotten this.

Brother Alec led them to the room of the missing sage, **Matreous**, where investigation revealed a hidden portal and the passphrase to open it. Inside they found Matreous, alive, clutching an imp statue. He thanked them, asked them to keep exploring while he returned to study the statue — and the moment he stepped back through the portal, they heard a scream. The portal closed behind him.

Trapped inside **Fistandia's Mansion**, the party worked their way through a strange extradimensional estate full of animated objects, dangerous magic, and the remnants of a wizard who drew her power from a pact with the goddess **Mystra**. They discovered Fistandia's homunculi — **Cumin** and **Coriander** — and eventually found the magic word that opened the exit: *Liberty.*

They emerged to find Matreous dead and an imp waiting for them. They killed it, collected their reward, and walked away with two small homunculi and the memory of gargoyle eyes following Luci across a temple.

## Session 2 — *Mazfroth's Mighty Digressions* | November 22, 2025

Back at Candlekeep, the Avowed had a new job. Books had been coming to life and killing people. The party was asked to find out where they came from.

The trail led to **Baldur's Gate** — specifically to a bookstall in **The Wide** called **Amberdune Books**. The workers gave the party an uncomfortable feeling they couldn't quite name, so they watched. They waited. They let the shift change happen — and then Luci followed the workers home.

He tailed them to their house and, using a distraction courtesy of some **tiefling children** in the street, slipped inside. He came out with stolen gold, stolen books, and something far more significant — the **heart of a Sphinx**, wrapped in black silk, sitting atop a pile of coins.

That night, Luci had a vision. The Sphinx spoke to him directly: return the heart to the jackalweres. He told the others. They went back.

What followed was tense — some fighting, some running, and eventually a conversation with **Korvala**, the pack's leader. The jackalweres weren't villains. They were loyal servants of a Sphinx named **Nidalia**, a keeper of knowledge who had died, and they had been selling the animated books to raise gold for her resurrection ritual. The party agreed to help. They returned the stolen gold — and then some — and prepared to take part in the ritual.

## Session 3 — *The Resurrection of Nidalia & The Book of the Raven* | February 1, 2026

The ritual required a price from each participant — a memory, freely given, in exchange for Nidalia's return.

**Nidalia** — a golden sphinx with a lion's body, great feathered wings, and an ancient, cryptic way of speaking — asked each of them a question before taking what was offered.

- **Morvax** asked: *"How do I become the most powerful wizard there ever was?"* He gave her his memory of the village fire. He no longer remembers burning his home.
- **Luci** asked: *"Will I end up in prison?"* He gave her the memory of Groga's rejection. It was also this session that Luci formally chose his path — the **Agent of Bhaal** subclass. Whether he understood what that meant is another question.
- **Ash** asked: *"When and how will I die?"* He gave her the memory of losing his virginity.

Nidalia was resurrected. Boons were granted. The party left Beregost with something new — a map, hidden inside **The Book of the Raven**, leading to a remote highland estate called **Chalet Brantifax**.

The road there passed through **Wytchway**, an abandoned village. It did not stay quiet.

*The party fought a battle there. They are still standing. The Chalet waits.*

# Faction — Keepers of Forbidden Lore

_The Hidden Hand Behind Candlekeep's Mysteries_

> *"Knowledge is a weapon. We choose who bleeds."*

![[Keeper_Seal.avif]]
> [!important] DM Eyes Only The Keepers of Forbidden Lore are the primary antagonist faction of this campaign. Their inner council members believe they serve a righteous cause. None of them — except Xanthoria — know the full truth of what they have built. This is the document that holds everything together. Keep it updated as the party uncovers more.

---

## Overview

The Keepers of Forbidden Lore are a secretive organisation that has operated in the shadows of Candlekeep for decades. Their stated philosophy: knowledge is a weapon, and weapons should only be held by those trained and worthy to wield them. They believe Candlekeep's open-access model is negligence — that allowing any scholar with a rare book to waltz in and study plague rituals, void entities, and world-ending artifacts is reckless beyond measure.

It is a compelling philosophy. Several of their members believe it sincerely.

Their true goal — known only to Xanthoria — is to reach the Liber Infinitus in the Chamber of Lost Lore beneath Candlekeep, seize it, and use its reality-reshaping knowledge to give Xanthoria dominion over life and death on a scale that would make her the most powerful being in Faerûn.

Everything else is cover.

---

## The Inner Council

The Keepers are governed by an inner council of four. No single member leads — decisions are made collectively, or so the others believe. In practice, Xanthoria has been steering the council's agenda from the beginning.

---

### Xanthoria

_Lichen Lich | The Secret Architect_

The true mastermind. Xanthoria presents herself to the inner council as a powerful and dedicated scholar — a lich who achieved undeath through sheer devotion to knowledge rather than fear of death. She is calm, patient, and long-sighted in ways that make the other council members trust her judgment implicitly.

What they don't know: the Saprophytic Plague, the mycelial psychic network, Janussi's infection — all of it is her work. The Keepers have never been anything more than her instrument.

**What she wants:** The Liber Infinitus. Dominion over life and death. **What she tells the council:** That their goal is to properly custodise what lies beneath Candlekeep under qualified guardianship — to do what the Avowed cannot. **Her phylactery:** Thunderwing, a living sprite. Cannot be harmed unless she chooses. **Her origin:** Zuggtmoy's influence began her corruption. She completed the transformation willingly.

See [[Xanthoria]] for full stat block and encounter notes.

---

### Verath Soln

_Human Male, Late 50s | The Financier_

A former merchant banker who discovered that rare knowledge was more profitable than rare goods. Verath manages the Keepers' finances, their legitimate business fronts, and their supply chains for moving acquired texts. He is charming, precise, and deeply practical — he has no ideology beyond the belief that proper resource management is its own form of virtue.

**What he does:** Funds operations, manages fronts, signs contracts with outside parties on behalf of the Keepers. He signed the formal contract with Valin Sarnaster in The Canopic Being. **What he believes:** That the Keepers are doing necessary work and that he is compensated fairly for enabling it. **His weakness:** He has no loyalty to anything that can't pay him back. If cornered and offered a better deal, he will take it. He is the council member most likely to flip — not out of conscience, but out of self-interest. **What he doesn't know:** Xanthoria's true nature. The plague. He would be genuinely alarmed if he found out — not because of the moral horror, but because he doesn't want to be connected to something that catastrophic.

---

### Illyra Voss

_Female Half-Elf, Early 40s | The Intelligence Chief_

Illyra recruits, places, and manages the Keeper agent network across the Sword Coast. She is the correspondent the party knows as "K." — that is her operational signature. She is brilliant, careful, and has never been seen in person by any agent below inner council level.

**What she does:** Runs the agent network. Places agents inside organisations. Writes the correspondence the party has been finding across multiple adventures. Manages the dead drop system. **What she believes:** That information is the only real power and that the Keepers are the only organisation using it responsibly. **Her weakness:** She is methodical to a fault. The party accumulating physical evidence of her correspondence is her greatest vulnerability — she leaves a paper trail because she believes her operational security is unbreakable. **What she doesn't know:** Xanthoria's true nature. She suspects something is off about Xanthoria's long-term plans but has not pressed the question.

> [!note] "K." Trail The party will have found Illyra's correspondence — signed only "K." — across multiple adventures before they learn her name. Vermeillon (A Deep and Creeping Darkness), Zikran's lab, and Bak Mei's sanctum all carry her handwriting. When they finally put the name to the signature, the moment should land.

---

### Dossivex

_Male Dragonborn (Black Dragon Ancestry), 60s | The Enforcer_

Dossivex is the council member who makes problems disappear. He has no particular interest in knowledge itself — he is interested in what knowledge enables, and in ensuring that obstacles to the council's goals are removed efficiently. He is the one who authorised the kill order on the Yellowcrest household in Sarah of Yellowcrest Manor.

**What he does:** Sends agents to handle physical problems. Authorises violence when the council determines it is necessary. Runs the acquisitions side of the operation — when negotiation fails, Dossivex finds another way. **What he believes:** That the Keepers' goal is legitimate and that he is the reason it remains achievable. He is pragmatic and unsentimental. **His weakness:** He is the council member the party will meet directly — in The Scrivener's Tale, bearing the Scrivener's Mark, in serious trouble. He has information. He is not above a temporary arrangement if it serves his survival. **What he doesn't know:** Xanthoria's true nature. He would not be as alarmed as Verath — he is comfortable working for powerful beings. But he would want to renegotiate his terms.

The Keepers operate on a strict need-to-know basis. No agent knows more than their handler requires them to know. The inner council is unknown to all but the most senior agents.

### Outside Candlekeep

Various low-level couriers, scholars, and merchants operate as Keeper assets across the Sword Coast. The party has found evidence of agents in: Vermeillon, Zikran's laboratory, the yuan-ti temple (Book of Cylinders), Yellowcrest Manor, Bak Mei's sanctum, and the Haven of the Red Quill.

## Their Plan — Act by Act

### Act One (Levels 1–5) — Mapping and Positioning

The Keepers are in intelligence-gathering mode. They are identifying what lies beneath Candlekeep, mapping approaches to Miirym, and acquiring tools that might neutralise her. The party finds only subtle traces — physical evidence that something is watching.

### Act Two (Levels 6–10) — Active Acquisition

The Keepers begin active operations. They tip off the yuan-ti about the Chime of Opening. They embed an agent in the Livestock cult during Kandlekeep Dekonstruktion. They commission research from Zikran and Bak Mei. Pell flags the Janussi signatures. The party starts to see the shape of something.

### Act Three (Levels 11–16) — Direct Action

The Keepers have concluded that workarounds won't work. The Wisteria Vale agent's notes confirm it: _"The Candlekeep approach must be direct. The guardian is the only remaining obstacle."_ They are now planning something overt. Their inner council members are personally involved. The party is finding named Keeper agents in the field.

### The Finale — Revelation

Xanthoria's true nature is exposed. The Keepers were always her instrument. The inner council members who survive must reckon with what they built.

## What the Keepers Know About Candlekeep

- The complete architectural layout of Candlekeep including underground passages (via the Dekonstruktion copies)
- Miirym's patrol patterns and known limitations (via Kei Tigersteel's observation notes)
- The existence of the Chamber of Lost Lore and the Liber Infinitus
- The Shielding Mythal and how to time operations around it
- Which Avowed are compromised, dissatisfied, or vulnerable
- Janussi can command Miirym — which is why infecting him was the priority

## What the Keepers Don't Know

- That the party is onto them
- That three of their four inside agents may be turned
- That Janussi, once freed, will command Miirym against them
- That Thunderwing is Xanthoria's phylactery (only Xanthoria knows this)

---

## The Keeper Seal

A wax seal used on all Keeper correspondence and official documents. The design: an open eye inside a closed book. The party will encounter it across multiple adventures before learning what it means. When they finally do — in Act Two, via the Yellowcrest kill order or the yuan-ti agent's collar insignia — everything behind them snaps into focus.

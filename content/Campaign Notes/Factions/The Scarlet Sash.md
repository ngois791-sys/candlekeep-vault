# Faction — The Scarlet Sash

_Wereravens of the Highland Chalet_

> [!note] Potential Allies The Scarlet Sash are not enemies. The party may have arrived at Chalet Brantifax expecting trouble and found instead a group of principled, cautious people trying to do good in a world full of dangerous objects. How the party treats them in Book of the Raven will determine the nature of this relationship going forward.

---

## Overview

The Scarlet Sash is a loosely organised order of wereravens founded by Anil Zasperdes — a wereraven who believed that dangerous magical items should be kept out of evil hands. Members of the Scarlet Sash seek out items of evil repute, acquire or steal them, and hide them in secure locations until they can be safely disposed of or kept permanently out of circulation.

They are not adventurers. They are not heroes for hire. They are careful, long-lived people who have committed themselves to a quiet and unglamorous kind of good.

The Sash currently has eight members, though it has had as few as three and as many as twelve in the past. They use Chalet Brantifax — a remote highland estate built for a long-dead baron — as their primary meeting place and storage location.

---

## Code and Values

- **Secrecy first.** The Sash's existence is not publicly known. They prefer it that way. They do not publicise their acquisitions or their locations.
- **Restraint.** Wereravens are reluctant to attack in raven or hybrid form — they fear spreading the curse to those who don't deserve it.
- **Generosity.** Wereravens share their wealth with those in need. In humanoid form, they give modestly and quietly.
- **Good faith.** They respect local laws and strive to do good whenever possible. They are not criminals, even when their methods involve theft — they steal from evil, not from the innocent.

## Members

### The Four at Chalet Brantifax

_(Party has met these four during Book of the Raven)_

**Madrina Natterask** — (She) Lawful Good Human Wereraven, age 55 A short, stocky glassblower with a modest collection of homemade bottles and flasks. She sees herself as the matriarch of this kindness. Speaks calmly and matter-of-factly. Scolds those who display bad manners. Can mimic the sound of breaking glass, which she uses as a distraction. _Personality:_ "I can be trusted with a secret and will carry that secret to the grave." _Ideal:_ "Without vigilance, evil runs amok." _Flaw:_ "I love sticking my beak into everyone's business."

## The Orcus Figurine

The figurine is currently in Vinique's possession, hidden under rubble in C14 (Baron's Loft) at Chalet Brantifax. Its properties:

- Prevents undead within 30 ft from being turned
- Prevents raising of the dead within 30 ft
- Can summon a wraith-like avatar of Orcus
- Smells of decay within 5 ft

The Keepers sent an agent to recover it — believing it could neutralise Miirym's spectral nature in the catacombs. The agent found the Chalet but not the figurine and left empty-handed (leaving behind boot prints, a torn scrap of parchment, and a crowbar).

The Sash doesn't know the Keepers want it. They don't know what the Keepers are. They just know that some things need to be kept away from everyone.

**If the party tries to take the figurine:** The Sash will resist. Diplomatically first — Madrina will want to know why they want it and what they plan to do with it. If the party's reasons are good, she may agree to keep it somewhere more secure, or even hand it over temporarily. She will not simply give it away.

**If the party warns the Sash about the Keeper agent:** The Sash takes this seriously. Madrina goes very still when she hears someone came looking for a specific item. She will want to move the figurine immediately and will ask the party for help deciding where.

## Building the Relationship

The Scarlet Sash starts as wary neutrals. By the end of Book of the Raven, they can become cautious allies — if the party treated them with respect rather than as obstacles.

**What the Sash can offer as allies:**

- Intelligence on dangerous magical items circulating in the Sword Coast — they have been watching the same shadow network the Keepers use, from the other side
- Safe houses and hiding locations for items the party needs to secure
- Wereraven scouts — ravens blend into any environment; they can surveil locations the party cannot
- Knowledge of items the Keepers have been pursuing — the Sash has noticed the same pattern of acquisitions from the wrong direction

**What they will NOT do:**

- Fight unless directly threatened — they are not soldiers
- Expose themselves publicly
- Hand over an item without understanding its destination

**The long game:** If the party maintains the relationship across multiple acts, the Sash can become one of the most useful intelligence assets they have — because they have been watching the shadow market for dangerous items for decades and they have noticed the Keepers' fingerprints on several acquisitions without knowing who the Keepers are. The party can give them a name for what they've been seeing.

## Anil Zasperdes — The Founder

_Deceased Wereraven | Founder of the Scarlet Sash_

Zasperdes founded the Sash and hid the treasure map in the Book of the Raven as a practical joke — he chose the book because anyone interested in its subject matter (Vistani lore, hidden places, secret routes) might also be interested in following the map. He was the kind of person who believed that the right people would find the right things at the right time.

He is long dead. Madrina knew him. She doesn't talk about him much, but when she does, her voice changes.

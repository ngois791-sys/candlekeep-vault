# Faction — Nidalia's Network

_The Sphinx's Eyes Across the Sword Coast_

> [!note] Ally Faction Nidalia is the party's most significant non-Candlekeep ally. Her network is not a formal intelligence organisation — it is a loose collection of loyal individuals who serve her out of genuine devotion. The party's relationship with her is already established. This file covers how her network functions and what it can offer.

---

## Overview

Nidalia is an ancient sphinx of lore based in Beregost, approximately four to five hours from Candlekeep. She is not the head of a faction in the traditional sense — she does not command, organise, or direct. She observes, collects, remembers, and occasionally acts.

Her "network" exists because she has lived long enough to accrue debts, loyalties, and contacts across the Sword Coast — and because the Amberdune Pack, who served her for years and helped resurrect her, continue to operate in her name. These are not soldiers or spies. They are devoted individuals who would do almost anything for her.

The party's relationship with Nidalia is already personal — she holds their memories and has granted them boons. She is, in the most meaningful sense, the party's oracle.

## Nidalia Herself

See [[Nidalia]] for her full NPC file — appearance, personality, speech patterns, visit logs, boons granted, and memories taken.

**Quick reference:**

- Ancient sphinx of lore; golden, formal, cryptic in speech
- Located in Beregost, 4-5 hours from Candlekeep
- Holds the party's memories (given willingly in exchange for boons)
- Memories taken: Luci's happiest memory, Morvax's village fire memory, Ash's first intimate memory
- Boons granted: to be tracked in [[Nidalia]] visit log
- Suspects something regarding Luci and Bhaal but has not confirmed it

**What she knows:** Nidalia has been alive long enough to have heard of the Keepers of Forbidden Lore — not the current iteration, but predecessor organisations with similar philosophies. She does not know the Keepers' current membership or operations. If the party asks her directly about an organisation that believes dangerous knowledge should be restricted to the worthy, she will have things to say.

**What she will share:** Nidalia does not give information freely. She gives it in exchange — for memories, for service, for answers to her own questions. She is not cruel about this. She is simply ancient and operates on an economy the party is still learning to navigate.

## The Amberdune Pack

The Amberdune Pack is a group of seven jackalweres who served Nidalia for years, maintaining her operation at Amberdune Books in Baldur's Gate and working toward her resurrection. They succeeded — with the party's help. They remain devoted to her.

### Korvala — Pack Leader

_Jackalwere | Neutral | Leader of the Amberdune Pack_

Korvala is intelligent, pragmatic, and deeply loyal to Nidalia. She ran Amberdune Books as a front operation for years without complaint. She is not warm — she is efficient. She respects competence and distrusts sentimentality.

**Her role in the network:** She coordinates the pack's activities on Nidalia's behalf, manages Amberdune Books (which continues to operate as a legitimate used bookshop), and serves as Nidalia's primary point of contact in Baldur's Gate.

**Her relationship with the party:** Complicated. Luci stole from them. The party then helped resurrect Nidalia. Korvala is capable of separating those two facts and operating professionally — but she has not forgotten the theft, and she will not forget it.

### The Six Pack Members

- **Avani** — jackalwere; reliable; handles deliveries and courier work
- **Inbar** — jackalwere; the pack's best fighter; rarely speaks
- **Marliza** — jackalwere; handles Amberdune Books' day-to-day with Korvala
- **Ramah** — jackalwere; the youngest; curious and occasionally reckless
- **Theryn** — jackalwere; meticulous record-keeper; Korvala's deputy
- **Zan** — jackalwere; contacts across the docks and lower city of Baldur's Gate

## What the Network Can Do

### Information

Nidalia is a sphinx of lore — she knows things. If the party comes to her with a specific question and offers appropriate exchange, she can provide context, history, and lore that no other source can. She is particularly useful for:

- Ancient history — organisations, artifacts, and events predating living memory
- Identifying patterns across the party's experiences that they haven't connected yet
- Confirming or denying suspicions about major mysteries

The Amberdune Pack, via Zan's dock contacts and Theryn's records, has access to commercial intelligence across Baldur's Gate — shipping manifests, unusual acquisitions, who's been buying what from which questionable sources.

### Safe Passage and Contacts

Korvala can arrange introductions, safe houses, or discreet transport within Baldur's Gate and along the Sword Coast trade routes. The pack's jackalwere nature means they can operate in places the party cannot without drawing attention.

### The Gingwatzim Ritual

Korvala knows the gingwatzim creation ritual — taught to her by Nidalia. This is not something she will share casually. But it exists, and in the right circumstances (the party needs an object duplicate for infiltration, for example), it could be an option.

## The Memory Economy

Nidalia trades in memories. This is not metaphor — she literally takes them and holds them. The party has already given three:

|Character|Memory Given|Current Status|
|---|---|---|
|Lucifurry|Happiest memory — stealing with his sister in the orphanage|Held by Nidalia|
|Morvax|The village fire — the day he burned his home and was exiled|Held by Nidalia; he no longer remembers it|
|Vyr'ashen (Ash)|Losing his virginity|Held by Nidalia|

**These memories are not gone forever.** Nidalia holds them. They could, in principle, be returned. This is worth keeping in mind as the campaign progresses — if the moment is right, the return of a memory could be significant. Particularly Morvax's village fire, which he has entirely forgotten and which shapes who he is.

**Future exchanges:** When the party visits Nidalia again, she may want more memories in exchange for information or boons. The party will have to decide what they're willing to part with — and what losing it will mean.

## Visiting Nidalia

The party can visit Nidalia between adventures. Beregost is 4-5 hours from Candlekeep — a reasonable day trip.

**What a visit typically involves:**

- Nidalia greets them formally, in the manner of ancient things that are pleased to have interesting guests
- She asks what they have learned since their last visit — she is genuinely curious
- Exchange: information for information, memory for boon, question for question
- She will not answer every question directly. She answers in ways that point the party toward the answer themselves.

**Things she might tell them across the campaign:**

- Act One: She has heard of an organisation that believes knowledge should be restricted to the worthy. She heard of it a very long time ago. It did not end well then either.
- Act Two: She knows what a lichen lich is. She has never met one. She knows what they want. She will not share this for free.
- Act Three: She knows the name Liber Infinitus. She will not say how. The price for what she knows about it will be significant.

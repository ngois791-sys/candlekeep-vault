# The Copper Quill — Keeper Safe House

_Baldur's Gate | Upper City | Act One Climax_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only This is the Act One climax. The party has been finding Keeper fingerprints across six adventures without understanding what they were looking at. The Copper Quill is where it all clicks into place — they finally have a name for the seal, a name for the correspondent, and one name from inside Candlekeep. The Keepers knew they were coming and left in a hurry. That fact is as important as anything in the files.

---

## Overview

**Name:** The Copper Quill — Manuscript Services & Document Preparation **Location:** Upper City, Baldur's Gate — a respectable side street off the Wide, two blocks from the Seatower district **Front:** A legitimate scribe's office offering copy work, manuscript preparation, document authentication, and correspondence services for merchants, scholars, and legal firms **Reality:** A Keeper of Forbidden Lore operational hub — the primary node for managing agents, moving correspondence, and coordinating acquisitions across the Sword Coast

The Copper Quill has operated for eleven years. It is a real business with real clients. Its legitimate work has always been real — the cover is not a fiction, it is a functioning enterprise. This is what has kept it invisible for over a decade.

---

## How the Party Finds It

The discovery is a combination of evidence gathered across Act One converging on a single address. No single clue leads here alone — it is the accumulation. The party needs at least two threads to point them to Baldur's Gate. Three makes the address airtight.

---

**Thread 1 — Pell's irregular copy requests** _Source: Joy of Extradimensional Spaces (Level 1)_

After the party connects with Pell as an informant, he shares something he has been sitting on. Certain copy requests submitted to the House of the Binder over the past few years list a Baldur's Gate scribe's office as the return address for completed documents — The Copper Quill, Upper City, off the Wide. Legitimate on its face; researchers often use outside scribes for overflow work. But the specific requests routed through this address all touch restricted or sensitive texts. The request that directed Matreous toward Fistandia's book was one of them. Pell can provide the exact address.

> [!note] When This Becomes Available Pell shares this once the party has established him as a contact and demonstrated they are genuinely investigating something. He has been waiting for someone to ask the right question. Once the party starts asking about the copy request that led to Matreous's death, this comes out naturally.

---

**Thread 2 — The Keeper inquiry letter (Amberdune Books)** _Source: Mazfroth's Mighty Digressions (Level 2)_

If the party returns to Amberdune Books after Nidalia's resurrection — or if they searched the office thoroughly during the adventure — they can find old correspondence in Korvala's files. Among the rejected business enquiries is a letter politely asking whether Amberdune Books could source a copy of Castanamir's Guide to Gingwatzims. The letter is signed with only a seal — an open eye inside a closed book — and lists a return address: The Copper Quill, Upper City, Baldur's Gate.

The party recognises the seal. They were standing in Baldur's Gate when this letter was written and sent. The Keepers have been operating out of the same city since before the party's first adventure.

> [!note] When This Becomes Available This requires the party to either have searched Korvala's office during Mazfroth's, or to deliberately return to Amberdune Books once they start investigating the seal. Korvala will let them look through old rejected correspondence if asked — she has no attachment to it.

---

**Thread 3 — The Vermeillon waystation document** _Source: A Deep and Creeping Darkness (Level 4)_

The old water-damaged Keeper document found in the hidden compartment in Vermeillon references "a shipment of texts from the Keep" and lists a Baldur's Gate contact address for acknowledgment of receipt. The address is partially obscured by water damage but legible enough with a DC 12 Investigation check: Upper City, off the Wide. Cross-referenced with Thread 1 or Thread 2, it confirms the same location.

The document also carries a partial wax seal impression — the same open eye inside a closed book — now clearly matching what the party has been finding across other adventures.

> [!note] When This Becomes Available The party finds this during A Deep and Creeping Darkness if they search the hidden compartment in the mayor's mansion or the mine office. They may not understand its significance immediately. It becomes a thread when they start actively looking for the pattern.

---

**Thread 4 — The delivery note (Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme)** _Source: Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme (Level 4)_

The delivery note found tucked into the seam of Shemshime's protective case (DC 14 Investigation) confirms the book was sent from a Baldur's Gate address to an inside contact at Candlekeep. The handwriting is precise and careful — the same hand the party will eventually recognise as "K." The return address is The Copper Quill, Upper City, Baldur's Gate.

This is also the thread that confirms someone inside Candlekeep accepted this delivery deliberately — and that the Baldur's Gate address is not a random intermediary but the source of the operation.

> [!note] When This Becomes Available Found during Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme if the party examines the book's protective case during the adventure. Easy to miss in the chaos of the quarantine — but if Pell's thread is already active, the party may specifically look for an address on anything connected to the Keeper seal.

---

> [!important] How Many Threads Do They Need? Two threads pointing to the same Baldur's Gate address is enough to send the party there. Three makes the case airtight. Four is confirmation of a sustained, coordinated operation. Give them what they've earned through play — don't hand them anything they didn't find themselves. The Copper Quill should feel like a discovery they made, not a destination they were handed.

---

## The Building

A narrow three-story building with a copper quill sign hanging above the door — the quill is old and has turned green with oxidation, which nobody has bothered to fix. Ground floor is the public office. Second floor is staff workspace and records. The third floor is a private office that doesn't appear on any city registry document.

**Ground Floor — Public Office** A reception counter, three writing desks, shelves of reference texts, samples of calligraphy styles. Smells of ink and paper. Everything is orderly and professional. A small bell on the counter. The kind of place that has a bowl of wrapped sweets near the door.

When the party arrives, the front door is unlocked. The reception area is empty. A half-drunk cup of tea sits on the counter — still faintly warm. The sweets bowl has been knocked over, wrapped candies scattered across the floor.

**Second Floor — Staff Workspace** Four writing desks. Shelving with client files — all legitimate. A locked door at the far end of the hall leads to the third floor. The lock has been changed recently — the doorframe still shows fresh scrape marks where the old hardware was removed in a hurry.

DC 14 Dex with thieves' tools, or DC 17 Strength to force. The door is solid.

**Third Floor — The Keeper Office** This floor does not exist on any public record. The staircase is hidden behind a false bookshelf panel on the second floor (DC 13 Investigation to find; the shelf swings inward).

This is where the party finds everything.

---

## The Third Floor — What They Find

The room was working until very recently. Someone was here within the last few hours. The fireplace has warm ash and the remnants of burned documents — not completely destroyed. The desk has been cleared but not wiped. Several empty document folders remain, their contents taken. One drawer was left open in the rush.

---

### 1. The Keeper Seal — Physical Stamp

A heavy iron seal stamp sitting on the desk — too bulky to grab quickly when evacuating in a hurry. The design: an open eye inside a closed book. Wax and an inkpad beside it.

This is the first time the party has the seal in their hands as a physical object rather than as an impression on a document. They now know exactly what they've been looking at across six adventures.

> [!note] The Weight of This Moment Give this a beat. The party has seen this seal on the Yellowcrest kill order, on the yuan-ti agent's collar, on documents in Vermeillon. They've been carrying fragments of this symbol for sessions without a name for it. Let them sit with having it in their hands.

---

### 2. "K." — The Correspondence File

A leather correspondence folder left in the open drawer. Inside: eleven letters, all in the same precise handwriting the party has seen before. Signed only "K."

The letters cover the following operations — confirming the Keeper fingerprints the party has already found:

- A letter directing an agent to research Fistandia's mansion and ensure Matreous was steered toward it _(Joy of Extradimensional Spaces)_
- A letter enquiring about acquiring Castanamir's Guide from Amberdune Books, and a follow-up noting the attempt was declined _(Mazfroth's Mighty Digressions)_
- An agent's report (received, not sent) confirming the Orcus Figurine was not recovered from Chalet Brantifax — _"the wereravens have secured it. Recommend monitoring."_ K.'s response: _"Noted. Abandon the site. The figurine is a secondary priority."_ _(Book of the Raven)_
- A letter authorising the Vermeillon waystation and its subsequent shutdown when meenlock activity made it untenable _(A Deep and Creeping Darkness)_
- An older letter (six years ago) authorising the placement of Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme inside Candlekeep — _"the window should create sufficient disruption for the retrieval operation. Ensure the inside contact confirms receipt."_ _(Shemshime's Bedtime Rhyme)_
- A payment arrangement with a _"Feywild-aligned coven in the High Forest"_ — texts delivered, treatments received, no names _(The Price of Beauty)_

The party now has documentary confirmation of Keeper involvement in every Act One adventure. And they know K. has been orchestrating all of it from this address.

**What's missing:** K.'s full name. K.'s physical description. Any inner council names except one.

---

### 3. The Ledger — Orris Vane

A financial ledger, partially burned in the fireplace. The party can recover approximately 60% of it with a careful DC 12 Investigation check — the spine held together and many pages survived the heat.

The ledger records payments and transfers across the Copper Quill's Keeper operations. Most names are coded. But one entry is not:

_"Transfer to V.O. at the Keep — three months advance. Reclassification services, ongoing. Reference: underground approach study."_

"V.O." appears six times across the ledger. A DC 14 Investigation check reveals the initial pattern: the entries began approximately eighteen years ago, correspond with large batches of reclassification activity at Candlekeep, and always note a Candlekeep-side contact.

V.O. The party may not have a name yet. But if they ever encounter Orris Vane — Senior Vault Archivist — and notice his initials, or if Pell eventually names him, the connection will snap into place.

> [!note] Dropping the Name Directly If the party is struggling or you want the reveal to land more cleanly, one of the partially burned pages can show the full notation: _"Vane, O. — vault reclassification, ongoing retainer."_ Your call on how explicit to make it.

---

### 4. The Burned Documents

In the fireplace: warm ash, partially destroyed papers. The party can recover fragments with a DC 15 Investigation check:

- A partial list of agent designations — code names only, no real names, but the party can see there are at least nine active agents referenced
- A fragment of a letter referencing _"the approach to the lower chambers"_ — the first hint that the Keepers' interest in Candlekeep goes underground, though no specifics survive
- A destroyed document that was clearly a map of some kind — only the corner remains, showing what looks like a scale marker and the word _"depth"_

These fragments are Act Two breadcrumbs. Enough to make the party hungry for more. Not enough to show them what's actually down there.

---

### 5. The Handler's Desk

The desk has been cleared. But the party can determine from the chair height, ink stains, and a faint impression on the blotter that one person worked here regularly — a left-handed writer who pressed hard. If the party has Ramilir's writing samples or comparison materials, a DC 16 Investigation check confirms this is not Ramilir's hand. This is someone else. Someone they haven't met.

On the blotter, under the impression of the last document pressed there, they can recover (DC 13 Investigation, using a charcoal rubbing): a partial address in Baldur's Gate's Outer City — a secondary location. You decide whether to develop this as a follow-up lead in Act Two.

---

## The Question the Party Should Be Asking

_Who tipped them off?_

The Keepers abandoned this location within hours of the party beginning to piece the evidence together. Someone inside Candlekeep — or very close to the party — alerted them.

The party cannot prove who. But they should be suspicious. The right answer is Serelyth — she overheard enough in the Hearth to understand the party was getting close and passed it along through her dead drop. She did not know this would cause anyone to flee. She thought she was passing routine intelligence. She is already regretting it, though she doesn't know the full consequences yet.

Do not confirm this to the party. Let them suspect. Let them look at everyone they trust inside Candlekeep differently from this point forward.

---

## The Aftermath

When the party leaves the Copper Quill, Act One is over. They have:

- The Keeper seal — physical, in hand
- K.'s correspondence — eleven letters, six adventures confirmed
- Orris Vane's initials in the financial ledger
- Confirmation that the Keepers have underground interests at Candlekeep
- The knowledge that someone warned the Keepers they were coming

They do not have:

- K.'s real name (Illyra Voss — Act Two)
- Inner council names beyond Vane's partial reference
- The catacomb plan in any detail
- Who warned the Keepers

This is the right amount to know at the end of Act One.

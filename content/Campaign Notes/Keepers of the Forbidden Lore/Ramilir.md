# Ramilir

_Keeper Agent — Operations (Coerced) | The Avowed, Candlekeep_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only Ramilir is not a Keeper. He is a good man in an impossible situation. He has been blackmailed for years and hates every second of it. If the party offers him a genuine way out, he breaks immediately and tells them everything. His sister was taken as leverage. Her current status is intentionally undetermined — the DM decides when the time is right.

## Appearance

A round-faced, soft-featured man who looks permanently tired. Not the tired of overwork — the tired of sustained, low-grade fear. His brown hair is going gray at the temples faster than it should for his age. His eyes are warm and attentive but carry something behind them that flickers when certain subjects come up: quick to change the subject, quick to look away.

He keeps his Avowed robes neat to the point of fastidiousness — a man who controls the small things because the large things have been taken from him. His hands are always busy: sorting, filing, straightening. When he's idle he looks lost.

**Voice:** Gentle and slightly apologetic in register, as though he's permanently braced to have said the wrong thing. Laughs easily but briefly, cutting himself off as if he doesn't feel he's earned it.

---

## Who He Is — The Truth

Ramilir has been an Avowed acolyte for nineteen years. He came to Candlekeep because he loved books — genuinely, simply, without ambition. He never sought advancement. He liked the quiet. He liked the smell of old paper. He liked knowing that what he did every day — cataloguing, filing, copying — kept something permanent safe.

Four years ago, his sister Mira disappeared.

A Keeper courier came to him three weeks later. They had her. They told him what they wanted. They were very polite about it. They made clear what would happen if he refused or if he told anyone.

He has been doing what they ask ever since. He misfiles copy requests. He moves books to incorrect shelf locations — not reclassified, just... misplaced, hard to find, a small delay in the wrong scholar's research at the right moment. He forged Janussi's signature on three copy requests during the Kandlekeep Dekonstruktion emergency. He processes copy requests from Keeper agents and destroys the records.

He goes home to his small room in the Exaltation every night and does not sleep well.

**What he doesn't know:** Almost nothing about the Keepers beyond his handler and the specific tasks he's given. He doesn't know about Xanthoria, the plague, or the catacombs plan. He has deliberately not asked. He is afraid that the more he knows, the more dangerous he becomes to them — and the more dangerous they become to him.

**Mira:** Her status is unknown. Ramilir has received three letters over four years, purportedly from her, confirming she is alive and unharmed. He doesn't know if the letters are real. He cannot verify. He does what he's told because he cannot afford to find out what happens if he doesn't.

---

## What He Does for the Keepers

- **Forgeries** — his handwriting is careful and practiced; he has forged Janussi's signature on at least three significant copy requests and likely more minor ones
- **Misfiled books** — a text moved to the wrong shelf can be unfindable for weeks; he has done this dozens of times to researchers whose work the Keepers wanted slowed
- **Copy request destruction** — he processes requests from Keeper agents and removes the records, making the copies untraceable
- **The Scrivener's Tale incident** — he accidentally gave the party the wrong book. This was a genuine mistake — he grabbed the wrong text from the vault — and it nearly exposed him. He spent three days in terror waiting to be punished. His handler was not pleased.

---

## The Scrivener's Tale — Was It Really an Accident?

**Yes.** A DC 10 Insight check in the published adventure confirms his distress is genuine — and it is. The accident was real. He was flustered, grabbed the wrong book, and didn't realise until it was too late.

However — in your connected campaign, the party may revisit this moment later with new information. They'll know he's an agent. They'll wonder. You can let that ambiguity sit. The truth is it was an accident, but the truth isn't always satisfying, and the party being uncertain is interesting.

---

## How He's Discovered

**Pell Ashwick** (Act One/Two informant, House of the Binder copyist) notices that certain copy requests Ramilir has processed don't match standard logs. Some have been filed, completed, and the records don't exist. The handwriting on the copy requests themselves is slightly inconsistent with the Avowed scribes who supposedly processed them. Pell is a copyist — he knows handwriting. He brings this to the party quietly. He doesn't want to believe it.

When the party connects this to the three Janussi signature forgeries Pell flagged during Kandlekeep Dekonstruktion — same irregularities, same handler — the picture becomes clear.

**The critical tell:** The Scrivener's Tale incident. The party now knows Ramilir was in the vault when the book was retrieved. After learning he's an agent, they may go back to that moment and wonder what else he's taken from the vault. Investigation of vault access logs reveals a pattern of entries with no corresponding legitimate requests.

---

## The Confrontation

Ramilir folds the moment the evidence is on the table.

He doesn't argue, doesn't deny, doesn't try to explain. He looks at whatever evidence the party has placed in front of him, and his face does something complicated, and then he asks one question: _"Can you actually get her back?"_

If the party says yes, or even maybe — he tells them everything. He is not a brave man. He has been carrying this alone for four years and he is exhausted by it. He will cry, probably. He will apologise repeatedly, not performing contrition but genuinely distressed by what he's done to people he respected.

**What he can give them:**

- His handler's name and drop location
- A full list of every copy request he processed, every book he misfiled, every forgery he committed — from memory, because he memorised them all in case he ever needed to prove his innocence
- Confirmation of which copy requests were for which Keeper agents (he sometimes recognised the requests as coming from the same source even without names)
- The three Janussi forgeries in full detail — what was requested, what the underground passage sections described

**What he cannot give them:**

- He doesn't know who the Keepers are above his handler. He has been carefully isolated.
- He doesn't know Mira's location.

**If offered no hope for his sister:** He still breaks — but he breaks differently. He is not protecting himself. He never was. If Mira is truly beyond reach, he has nothing to protect. He tells them everything and then asks if they're going to turn him in. He will accept whatever they decide.

---

## After the Confrontation

If the party keeps him in place as a double agent — feeding the Keepers false information — he is terrified but capable. He is, fundamentally, a good liar born of four years of practice. He will do it. He will not enjoy it. He will check in with the party as often as they let him.

If the party turns him over to the Avowed: he accepts it. He doesn't fight. He thanks them for stopping it.

If the party finds and rescues Mira (DM determines her status and location when ready): the scene between them when she's returned to him is worth giving space at the table. Don't rush it.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

- He is kind. Genuinely, reflexively kind — he holds doors, remembers names, asks after people's research with real interest. The Keepers picked him specifically because no one would suspect him. His kindness is not a mask. It's who he is, coexisting with what he's been forced to do.
- He knows Pell Ashwick is getting close to figuring it out. He likes Pell. This torments him.
- He has a small portrait of Mira in his room. He looks at it every morning before he goes to work.
- If the party has been kind to him across multiple sessions before the confrontation — and they probably have, because he's easy to like — the reveal will land harder. That's the point.

---

## Mira — Status Undetermined

> [!warning] DM Decision Required Mira's current status is intentionally left open. The DM will decide when the time is right whether she is:
> 
> - Alive and held somewhere recoverable
> - Alive but in a location that requires a significant quest to reach
> - Dead — and the Keepers have been sending forged letters
> 
> All three are valid. All three produce a different emotional outcome at the table. The decision should be made based on what your players need from this story at the moment it becomes relevant.

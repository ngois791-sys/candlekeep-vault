# Lorekeeper Kei Tigersteel

_Keeper Agent:  —  Inner Ward Access | Temple of Oghma, Candlekeep_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only Kei is a willing Keeper agent driven by genuine disillusionment with Oghma's passive approach to knowledge and its protection. He is not cruel, not malicious, and not beyond reach. He can be redeemed — but it requires the party to engage with his actual argument rather than simply defeating him. He is the most overtly spiritual of the four agents, and his faith — now misplaced — is the key to turning him.

## Appearance

A compact, precise man with close-cropped black hair going silver at the temples, warm brown skin, and the calm, measured bearing of someone who has spent decades in contemplative practice. He wears the purple vestments of a Deneir priest only on formal occasions — day to day he dresses simply in grey robes with a small bronze Oghma medallion at his collar.

His eyes are dark and attentive, with the quality of someone who looks at you long enough to form an actual opinion before speaking. He moves quietly. He has a habit of standing very still when listening, hands clasped loosely behind his back, head slightly tilted — an old scholar's posture.

**Voice:** Measured and warm, with a faint lilt from his Shou upbringing that becomes more pronounced when he's speaking about something he cares about. He never rushes. He treats questions as worthy of real consideration before answering.

## Who He Is — The Truth

Kei Tigersteel has served Oghma for thirty-one years. He came to Candlekeep as a young priest filled with conviction: the god of knowledge was the most important power in Faerûn, and his servants were doing the most important work. Preserving. Recording. Sharing.

The conviction eroded slowly, over decades.

He watched Oghma's faithful spend thirty years carefully documenting a spreading blight in the Western Heartlands — logging, categorising, cross-referencing — while the blight consumed four villages. He watched the church argue over whether a cure derived from forbidden alchemical texts was theologically permissible to use while people died of something that could have been stopped. He watched Candlekeep sit on top of the most complete body of dangerous knowledge in Faerûn and respond to every crisis with the same answer: _preserve and protect. Do not interfere. Let mortals work it out._

He stopped believing that was wisdom. He started believing it was cowardice dressed up as principle.

The Keepers found him twelve years ago through Serelyth — she had flagged him as dissatisfied, disillusioned, and close to leaving the church entirely. Their recruiter spent six months in conversation with him before asking for anything. They listened to his arguments. They agreed with them. They showed him an organisation that believed knowledge was a weapon and weapons should be held by those trained to use them.

He said yes.

**His specific value to the Keepers:** He is the only non-Avowed individual in Candlekeep with unaccompanied access to the library. The Avowed granted this privilege out of respect for his decades of service and scholarly contribution. He has used it to map the Inner Ward, identify which restricted texts have moved, assess Miirym's patrol patterns in the catacombs below, and report on which Avowed are working on what. He is the Keepers' best set of eyes in the most restricted space Candlekeep has.

**What he doesn't know:** He does not know about Xanthoria's true nature. He knows the Keepers have a goal related to the catacombs and believes it involves properly securing what lies there under appropriate custodianship. He does not know about the Saprophytic Plague or the Liber Infinitus by name. He does not know about Janussi's infection — he believes Janussi has been persuaded, not compromised.

## What He Does for the Keepers

- **Inner Ward surveillance** — reports on restricted vault contents, which texts have been moved or reclassified, which scholars are being granted elevated access and why
- **Miirym observation** — he has spent years quietly observing her patrol patterns in the upper catacomb levels accessible from the library; his notes on her behaviour and known limitations are the most detailed the Keepers possess
- **Keeper symbol placement** — he has marked two locations in the Inner Ward with the Keeper seal, in concealed spots only someone with unaccompanied access could reach; these serve as dead drops for passing information to Keeper couriers who gain Inner Ward access as visiting scholars
- **Vetting scholars** — he sits on an informal advisory body that reviews applications for elevated Inner Ward access; he has blocked two researchers whose work was getting too close to the catacombs and approved three Keeper-connected applications

## The Symbol Caelix Finds

In Act Three, Caelix Wren (informant, priest of Oghma — notably, Kei's own order) finds a Keeper symbol etched into stonework in the Inner Ward. He brings it to the party because he knows it doesn't belong there and because he's been watching for exactly this kind of evidence.

The symbol is in a location accessible only to someone with unaccompanied Inner Ward access. Cross-referencing access logs narrows the list to two individuals. One of them is a senior Avowed archivist with an airtight alibi for the time period when the symbol was placed. The other is Kei Tigersteel.

> [!note] Caelix's Complication Caelix is a priest of Oghma. Kei is a Lorekeeper of Oghma — his superior within the temple hierarchy. When Caelix realises who the symbol points to, he is shaken. He brings it to the party because he trusts them more than he trusts the church at this moment. He asks them to be careful. He asks them not to tell him what they find until they're sure.

---

## How He's Discovered

1. Caelix finds the Keeper symbol in the Inner Ward (Act Three trigger)
2. Access logs narrow the list to Kei
3. If the party investigates his quarters or his temple office, they find his observation notes on Miirym — written in a hand that doesn't match any Avowed research style, referencing details no casual visitor could have noticed
4. Confronting him with both pieces of evidence simultaneously is the cleanest approach — the symbol alone he could explain away; combined with the Miirym notes, he knows the conversation is over

## The Confrontation

Unlike the others, Kei does not immediately fold or immediately refuse. He asks the party to sit down. He asks if he can speak first before they decide what to do with him.

He tells them his history — not as a defence, but as context. He tells them what he watched happen in the Western Heartlands. He tells them about the villages. He tells them what it felt like to watch his god's church argue theology while people died. He tells them he stopped believing that preservation without action was virtue.

Then he asks them a question: _"What does Candlekeep do with the knowledge that it holds, beyond holding it?"_

He is not stalling. He genuinely wants to know if they have an answer.

**If the party engages with the question:** This is the path to redemption. Not immediately — he is not naive — but if they can demonstrate that knowledge in the right hands _does_ lead to action and protection, and if they show him evidence of what the Keepers actually are (plague, lich, the contract from The Canopic Being), they crack something in him. He has built his entire second faith on the assumption that the Keepers are moral actors with a better philosophy. If that's a lie, the philosophy doesn't save him.

He goes very quiet. He asks them to give him a day.

When he comes back, he helps them. He gives them his Miirym observation notes (legitimately useful in the finale), the dead drop locations, and every name he knows in the Keeper network. He then goes to the temple and prays for the first time in years — not to Oghma for guidance, but in apology.

**If the party threatens or rushes him:** He closes down. He is not afraid of them and he will not be bullied into a decision this significant. He tells them he needs time, and if they won't give it to him, they should do what they're going to do. He will accept the consequences.

**If the party turns him in without giving him the chance to hear the truth:** He accepts it. He does not beg. But there's a scene worth playing if Caelix is present — his junior in the faith, watching his Lorekeeper led away. Caelix doesn't say anything. That silence is loud.

## Roleplaying Notes

- He is genuinely devout — or was. He still uses the forms of his faith unconsciously: he blesses meals, he quotes Oghma's texts, he keeps the temple immaculate. The faith is in his bones even if he no longer believes the institution serves it.
- He is the only agent who, if redeemed, explicitly asks the party what they're going to do with the Liber Infinitus once this is over. He wants to know if they've thought about it. He has.
- He keeps a journal — not a spy's record, but a genuine spiritual diary. If the party finds it, it reads as the slow, agonising account of a man losing his faith in one thing and finding it in another. The Keeper section reads as hope, not conspiracy. This is important context for the confrontation.
- He and Caelix have a complicated relationship — Kei mentored him, respects him, and is quietly proud of him. He will not tell the party this. It shows.

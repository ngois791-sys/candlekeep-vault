# Serelyth Willowhush

_Keeper Agent — Intelligence | The Hearth, Candlekeep_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only Serelyth is a willing Keeper agent. She is not evil. She is a true believer who has drawn the wrong conclusions from the right observations. She can be redeemed — but only if the party gives her the truth about Xanthoria before they give her an ultimatum.

## Appearance

A tall, slender moon elf with pale silver-blue skin and hair like smoothed winter frost, pulled into a loose braid draped over one shoulder. Her eyes are a muted amethyst — warm and gentle rather than piercing. She dresses simply but gracefully: soft beige linens, embroidered cuffs, a moss-green apron tied neatly at the waist. She moves with quiet, fluid certainty, as though every step has already been considered before she takes it.

She is always doing something with her hands — polishing a glass, straightening a cloth, setting a cup down — and yet she always seems to be listening.

**Voice:** Low, velvety, and slow-paced with a faint melodic cadence. She speaks as though gently smoothing wrinkles from cloth. Brief pauses between sentences. Never hurried. Never rattled.

---

## Who She Is — The Truth

Serelyth has worked behind the bar of the Hearth for sixty-three years. She has listened to thousands of scholars, sages, seekers, and adventurers pass through — and for sixty-three years, she has watched the same pattern repeat itself.

A scholar spends six weeks in the library gaining access to knowledge that could change the world. They take notes. They leave. Nothing changes. The knowledge goes back on the shelf. The world stays broken.

She watched a historian spend a month studying ancient plague texts that could have saved a village — and publish a monograph no one read. She watched a wizard spend years studying wards that could protect refugees — and lock the findings in his tower. She watched Candlekeep itself sit on top of knowledge that could feed, heal, and defend thousands — and grant access only to those who could afford a rare enough book to get through the gate.

The Keepers of Forbidden Lore found her forty years ago. They told her they believed in something different: that knowledge should go to those who would _act_ on it, who had proven themselves worthy through dedication and sacrifice. They didn't offer her money. They offered her a philosophy she'd already arrived at on her own.

She has been feeding them intelligence ever since. Conversations overheard at the bar. Names of scholars researching sensitive topics. Which Avowed are ambitious, dissatisfied, or lonely. Who is close to finding something the Keepers want suppressed or redirected. She is, in every sense, the best intelligence asset the Keepers have inside Candlekeep — because she is trusted absolutely, has been there for decades, and has never once acted suspiciously.

**What she doesn't know:** She has never met Xanthoria. She knows the Keepers have an inner council she has no access to. She believes the organisation is run by dedicated scholars and activists. She does not know her handler is connected to a lichen lich whose goal is to corrupt Candlekeep with plague and seize a book that could unmake the world. If she knew, she would stop. Immediately.

## What She Does for the Keepers

- **Listens** — the Hearth is where every seeker, adjutant, and visiting scholar relaxes and speaks freely. She has sixty years of overheard conversations catalogued in her memory.
- **Identifies targets** — scholars who are close to sensitive discoveries, Avowed who are dissatisfied, visitors who are asking the wrong questions.
- **Reports** — she passes information via a dead drop in the Outer Ward (a loose stone behind the Temple of Oghma; messages retrieved weekly by a Keeper courier who poses as a traveling merchant).
- **Misdirects** — occasionally steers conversations subtly. Introduces doubts. Suggests a scholar's research is already well-covered elsewhere. Nothing dramatic. Barely perceptible.
- **Watches the party** — from the moment they arrive, she is paying attention. Not with suspicion. With professional interest.

---

## What She Knows

- The names and general roles of several Keeper agents inside and outside Candlekeep (not all — she's on a need-to-know basis)
- The location of the dead drop behind the Temple of Oghma
- That the Keepers have been trying to access something beneath Candlekeep — she knows it's significant but not the specifics
- That Janussi has been "guided" by the Keepers — she believes this means he has been persuaded and recruited, not infected
- The Keeper seal and recognition phrase

**She does not know:** Xanthoria exists. The Saprophytic Plague. The Liber Infinitus by name. What happens to agents who become liabilities.

---

## How She's Discovered

Serelyth is not discovered through evidence. She's discovered through pattern recognition — and only if the party is paying attention across multiple sessions.

**The tells:**

- She always knows things she shouldn't. When the party mentions something sensitive in the Hearth, the right NPC always seems to find out shortly after.
- A DC 18 Insight check during any conversation where she's actively listening — not speaking — reveals that her stillness is not calm. It's concentration.
- If the party uses the private meeting chambers (accessible from inside the Hearth), they may eventually notice that conversations held there don't seem as private as advertised. The geometric amplifier orb in the ceiling doesn't just expand the space — it amplifies sound throughout the building. Serelyth has known this for sixty years.

**The confirmation:** If the party investigates the dead drop (perhaps after trailing a Keeper courier), they find her handwriting on a message. There's no ambiguity. It's her.

> [!note] Pacing Don't reveal this until Act Two at the earliest — ideally Act Three. The party should have trusted her, relaxed around her, told her things. The retroactive chill of realising what those conversations cost them is the point.

---

## The Confrontation

Serelyth does not panic when confronted. She sets down whatever is in her hands, looks at the party evenly, and tells the truth. All of it. She is not ashamed of her beliefs — she is ashamed of nothing until the moment the party shows her what the Keepers actually are.

**If the party confronts her without evidence of Xanthoria:** She argues her position with quiet conviction. She asks them: how many scholars have they watched learn something important and do nothing? She's not cruel about it. She genuinely wants them to understand. She will not give up her contacts.

**If the party shows her evidence of Xanthoria — the plague, the contract, the truth:** She goes very still. She asks one question: _"Is this real?"_

If they convince her, she helps them. Completely. Everything she knows — names, the dead drop, what she's told the Keepers about the party, all of it. She doesn't ask for forgiveness. She asks what she can do.

**If the party gives her an ultimatum before giving her the truth:** She refuses. She has principles. Threats don't move her. Truth does.

---

## Roleplaying Notes

- She is never flustered. Even when the party suspects her, she is composed.
- She genuinely likes the party. This is not performance. She finds them interesting — more interesting than most seekers she's served. This makes the confrontation more complicated for everyone.
- She has a habit of refilling glasses before they're empty. A small, attentive gesture that suddenly reads differently once the party knows.
- After the reveal — if she helps the party — she continues working the bar. She has nowhere else to go. And someone needs to make sure the Keepers don't know their intelligence source has flipped.

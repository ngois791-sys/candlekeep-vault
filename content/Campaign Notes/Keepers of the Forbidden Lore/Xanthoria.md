# Xanthoria

_Lichen Lich | The Secret Architect | Final Boss_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only Xanthoria is the true villain of the campaign — the secret inner council mastermind of the Keepers of Forbidden Lore. She presents herself to the council as a dedicated scholar who achieved undeath through devotion to knowledge. None of them know she is a lichen lich, or that the Saprophytic Plague is her creation. She has been steering the campaign from behind the curtain since the beginning.

---

## Overview

|**Source**|Candlekeep Mysteries p. 223|
|**Adventure**|Xanthoria (Level 16 — Finale)|

Xanthoria was a half-elf druid of Silvanus whose forest home was threatened by undead. Her initial research into fungi and lichen was genuinely motivated — she wanted to protect her forest. At some point, Zuggtmoy's influence found her. Her research darkened. She turned from creating weapons against undead to studying undeath itself, then to achieving it. She transformed herself into a lichen lich — an undead druid — and discovered how to house her soul in a living creature rather than an inanimate object.

The living phylactery: a sprite named Thunderwing.

---

## Appearance

A robed half-elf figure with a small symbol of Silvanus hanging around her neck — a vestige of who she was. Up close: a skeleton covered with fungi and bark-like lichen. Vines writhe within her chest cavity, exuding viscid and poisonous black fluid.

She is quite lonely. She was expecting the party. She will talk to them before she fights them.

## Traits

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If Xanthoria fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.

**Rejuvenation.** If she has a phylactery, a destroyed Xanthoria gains a new body in 1d10 days, regaining all hit points and becoming active again. The new body appears within 5 feet of the phylactery — i.e., within 5 feet of Thunderwing.

## Actions

**Multiattack.** Xanthoria makes four attacks.

**Poisonous Touch.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +9 to hit, reach 10 ft., one creature. _Hit:_ 17 (5d6) poison damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 19 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 minute. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.

**Wither.** _Ranged Spell Attack:_ +9 to hit, range 60 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 14 (4d6) necrotic damage.

**Fire Storm (7th-Level Spell; 1/Day).** Xanthoria fills up to ten 10-foot cubes with fire. Every cube must be within 150 feet and adjacent to at least one other cube. Each creature in the area must make a DC 19 Dexterity saving throw, taking 38 (7d10) fire damage on a failed save or half on a success. The fire ignites flammable objects not worn or carried. If Xanthoria chooses, plant life in the area is unaffected.

**Spellcasting.** Wisdom as spellcasting ability (save DC 19):

- _At will:_ Druidcraft
- _3/day each:_ Detect Magic, Fog Cloud, Pass Without Trace
- _1/day each:_ Antilife Shell, Dispel Magic, Speak with Plants, Transport via Plants

## Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), Xanthoria takes one lair action:

- **Poisonous Spores.** Fill a 10-foot cube within 120 ft. Any creature entering or starting its turn there: DC 19 Con save or poisoned until end of next turn. Lasts 1 minute or until dispersed by strong wind.
- **Shambling Mound.** Create a shambling mound in an unoccupied space within 30 ft. Acts on its own initiative, obeys Xanthoria's commands. Dies after 1 hour or when this action is used again.
- **Rotten Roots.** Roots and vines erupt in a 20-foot radius centered on a point within 120 ft. Difficult terrain; creatures must succeed on DC 19 Strength save or be restrained. An action and DC 19 Strength (Athletics) check frees a restrained creature. Roots wilt when the lair action is used again or Xanthoria dies.

## Legendary Actions

The lich can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature’s turn. The lich regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

**Attack.** The lich makes an attack.

**Poison Prick (Costs 2 Actions).** The lich targets one poisoned creature it can see within 30 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 19 Constitution saving throw or fall unconscious until the poisoned condition ends on it.

**Sap Life (Costs 2 Actions).** The lich targets one creature it can see within 30 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 19 Constitution saving throw or take 11 (2d10) necrotic damage. The lich regains a number of hit points equal to the amount of damage that the creature takes.

## The Grove of Horrors — Environmental Factors

The area warded against magical travel by creatures other than Xanthoria. Creatures cannot teleport into or out of the area or use planar travel to enter or leave it. (These effects function if both origin and destination are within the area.)

## Encounter Notes

**When the party arrives:** Xanthoria senses them as soon as they enter the Lykortha Expanse (or in your campaign — the corrupted Great Library). She has been expecting them. She is willing to talk. She cannot be persuaded to stop her work. She has called a nalfeshnee demon (Danjak) and has a rotting infected treant and four invisible will-o'-wisps ready to defend her.

**The opening move:** She uses the antilife shell — activated by stealing the life force of a victim. She resumes working calmly while the spell is active.

**The Lost Memento:** Showing Xanthoria her old recipe book (with sketches of Thunderwing and Bunny Blossom in the margins) awakens dormant memories of her previous life. She takes no actions, legendary actions, or lair actions until the end of her next turn. This works only once.

**The Spore Trap:** Characters exposed to spores in area L4 (the Moldy Bedroom) who did not wash them off face a DC 20 Con save (50 necrotic damage on fail, half on success) at the start of their first turn in Xanthoria's grove.

## Thunderwing — The Phylactery

Thunderwing is a living sprite and Xanthoria's phylactery. She cannot be harmed by weapons or harmful effects unless she chooses to be — they pass through her as though she were a projection. She is subject to effects that turn undead.

**To permanently destroy Xanthoria:** Thunderwing must choose to sacrifice herself. She cannot be forced. She cannot be harmed. The party must convince her.

**Ways to convince Thunderwing:**

- Promise to raise Bunny Blossom (the yellow faerie dragon) from the dead
- Promise to complete Xanthoria's original mission — rid the surrounding forest of undead
- Destroy Xanthoria's notes so the lichen lich process can never be duplicated

**If convinced:** Thunderwing lets out a deep sigh. She flies about and kisses each character on the forehead — even the ones she doesn't like. She goes to the character she is closest to, lies in their open hands, and shivers slightly before expiring. Thunderwing cannot be brought back to life by any means.

**If not convinced:** Thunderwing turns invisible and flees. Xanthoria re-forms 1d10 days later, appearing next to Thunderwing. She relocates and the plague continues.

**Thunderwing's personality:**

- Trait: "I'm very emotional."
- Ideal: "Living things should be free and wild, not caught up in the trappings of civilization."
- Bond: "I will always try to help a friend."
- Flaw: "I'm such a scatterbrain. I forget important pieces of information until something reminds me."

## What Xanthoria Knows

- Everything about the Keepers of Forbidden Lore — she built them
- The location of the Liber Infinitus in the Chamber of Lost Lore
- Janussi's infection and the mycelial network
- The full extent of the Saprophytic Plague

## What Xanthoria Does Not Know

- That several of her inside agents may already be turned
- That the party has been collecting evidence against the Keepers for sessions
- That Janussi, once freed, will command Miirym against her

---

## After Her Defeat

The destruction of Xanthoria and Thunderwing halts the spread of the Saprophytic Plague. Infected creatures continue to suffer its effects but no longer speak her name and are no longer contagious. Their mycelial connection to her is severed.

The final few pages of her notes (found among her possessions) detail her growing obsession with undead and her success at turning Thunderwing into her phylactery.

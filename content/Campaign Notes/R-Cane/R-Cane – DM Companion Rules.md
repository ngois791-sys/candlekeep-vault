# R-Cane — DM Companion Rules

_Addiction Tracking, Recovery, and Tier Escalation_

> [!note] What This File Is This is the DM-side companion to [[R-Cane Information and Effects]]. It covers the rules that were missing from the main document: how addiction ends, exactly when mutation tiers escalate, and per-player tracking. The main file handles the effects — this file handles the consequences.

---

## Recovery — How Addiction Ends

Addiction to R-Cane is not a disease. It is a dependency written into the spell lattice itself. Standard disease-curing magic does not remove it outright.

### Option 1 — Abstinence (The Hard Way)

A character attempting to break addiction must go **14 consecutive in-game days without a dose**.

Each day of abstinence, the character makes a **DC 12 Wisdom saving throw**.

- **Success:** The day passes. Withdrawal symptoms apply normally (1 level of exhaustion, disadvantage on spell attacks, no long rest benefit).
- **Failure:** The craving wins. The character must either take a dose or the DM can decide they white-knuckle it — but the 14-day counter resets to zero.

After 14 successful consecutive days, the addiction breaks. Mutation tier effects fade over the following week (cosmetic changes may linger longer — DM's call).

> [!note] Roleplaying This This is where the party may have to intervene. A character in withdrawal is irritable, exhausted, and physically suffering. The 14-day arc is a story beat, not just a mechanic.

### Option 2 — Magical Intervention (The Expensive Way)

A **greater restoration** spell cast on an addicted creature does not cure the addiction but suppresses all withdrawal symptoms and cravings for **72 hours**. This buys time — it doesn't solve the problem.

A true magical cure requires either:

- A **wish spell** (the nuclear option)
- A specially prepared arcane purgative requiring rare components — this can be a quest hook. Nidalia would know the formula.

### Option 3 — The Nidalia Route (The Narrative Way)

Nidalia is a sphinx of lore with knowledge of arcane pharmacology that predates most written records. She knows exactly what R-Cane is, what it does to the spell lattice, and how to undo it.

The cost will not be cheap. She will want a memory — and not a small one.

## Mutation Tier Escalation — Clear Triggers

The main file lists vague escalation conditions. These are the exact mechanical thresholds.

|Tier|Trigger Condition|
|---|---|
|**None**|Character has used R-Cane but is not yet addicted|
|**Tier 1**|Addiction is established (failed DC 15 Wis save after 3 uses in 7 days)|
|**Tier 2**|Character has overdosed **3 times total** OR has been addicted for **30+ in-game days**|
|**Tier 3**|Character has overdosed **6 times total** OR has been addicted for **90+ in-game days**|

### DM Override

You can escalate a tier at any dramatically appropriate moment — a major emotional breakdown, a character doing something terrible while under the influence, or a pivotal story beat. You don't need to wait for the mechanical threshold. But the thresholds are the floor, not the ceiling.

### Descending Tiers

When an addiction is broken via abstinence:

- Tier 3 → Tier 2 after 14 days sober
- Tier 2 → Tier 1 after another 14 days
- Tier 1 → No tier after another 14 days

Cosmetic effects (skin changes, glowing veins, permanent sparks) may linger for weeks or be permanent at Tier 3, at the DM's discretion.

## Multi-Color Addiction

If a character is addicted to more than one color, each color tracks its tier separately. However:

- **Two active addictions:** -2 to all Wisdom saves (stacks on top of existing penalties)
- **Three active addictions:** The character's spell lattice is critically stressed. All spellcasting requires a DC 13 Con save or the spell fizzles. Non-spellcasters suffer nightmares and cannot benefit from long rests unless they take at least one dose of any color.
- **Mixing colors while addicted:** The mixing effects table still applies, but overdose checks are made at **DC 17** instead of DC 15.

## Quick Reference — The Full Addiction Loop

```
First use → acute effects only

3 uses within 7 days → DC 15 Wisdom save
  Success → not addicted (reset weekly counter)
  Failure → ADDICTED (Tier 1 mutations active)

While addicted:
  Every 48 hours without a dose → withdrawal begins
    1 level exhaustion
    Disadvantage on spell attacks
    No long rest benefit for 24 hours

Overdose (more than 1 dose per hour):
  Color-specific overdose effect triggers
  Add 1 to overdose count
  At 3 overdoses → Tier 2 (if not already there)
  At 6 overdoses → Tier 3

Breaking addiction (abstinence route):
  14 consecutive days without a dose
  DC 12 Wisdom save each day (fail = reset counter)
  After 14 days → addiction broken
  Tiers descend 1 per 14 additional sober days
```

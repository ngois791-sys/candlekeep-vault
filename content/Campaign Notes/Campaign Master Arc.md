# Campaign Master Arc

_Candlekeep Mysteries — Connected Campaign_

> [!important] DM Eyes Only This document contains the full shape of the campaign. Do not share with players.

---

## The Big Picture

Candlekeep is dying from the inside, and nobody knows it yet.

A lichen lich named **Xanthoria** has embedded herself as a secret leader within a shadowy criminal organization called the **Keepers of Forbidden Lore**. She is using them as a tool to gain access to **The Liber Infinitus** — an ancient book older than Candlekeep itself, locked in the catacombs beneath the keep, guarded by the spectral silver dragon **Miirym**. The book contains knowledge that could unmake or reshape the world, and Xanthoria believes it holds the final key to perfecting her research into dominion over life and death.

Her method is elegant and invisible. During a research expedition to the **Lykortha Expanse**, Xanthoria infected **Janussi** — the Keeper of Tomes and highest-ranking member of the Avowed — and several other senior Avowed with the **Saprophytic Plague**. The plague creates a mycelial psychic network between Xanthoria and the infected, allowing her to slowly replace their thoughts with her own. Janussi does not know he is compromised. He believes every thought is his own.

The Keepers of Forbidden Lore think they have a corrupt inside contact. They have no idea their own inner council member is a lichen lich who created that corruption — and who plans to infect and control every single one of them in time.

The party runs Candlekeep Mysteries as bounties from the Avowed. They are solving problems. What they do not yet know is that every problem they solve has the Keepers' fingerprints on it — and behind the Keepers, something far older and far worse is pulling threads.

## The Liber Infinitus

_"The infinite book — older than Candlekeep, older than the road that leads to it."_

|Property|Detail|
|---|---|
|**Location**|Catacombs beneath Candlekeep — the Chamber of Lost Lore|
|**Guardian**|Miirym the Sentinel Wyrm (spectral silver dragon)|
|**Contents**|Knowledge that could unmake or reshape the world|
|**Who Wants It**|Xanthoria (to perfect her research), The Keepers (to monopolize and profit), and eventually the party (to protect or destroy it)|
|**Current Status**|Secured — Miirym is on guard, Candlekeep's wards are intact|

The Liber Infinitus predates Candlekeep. How it came to rest in the catacombs is itself a mystery — the Avowed's oldest records simply note that it was there when the first scholars arrived. The Keeper of Tomes is the only one who knows of its existence. Janussi knows. And through Janussi, so does Xanthoria.

Miirym cannot be defeated in traditional combat — she is spectral, bound, and unkillable by conventional means. The only way past her is through the Keeper of Tomes, who alone can command her to stand down. While Janussi is compromised, Xanthoria has indirect influence over whether Miirym acts. Once Janussi is cured and freed, he can direct Miirym — and in the final confrontation, he does.

> [!warning] DM Note The party should not learn the Liber Infinitus exists until late in the campaign. Let them piece together that there is _something_ in the catacombs the Keepers desperately want — and let the name and nature of it land as a revelation, not a premise.

## The Three Factions

|Faction|Goal|Knows About...|
|---|---|---|
|**[[Keepers of Forbidden Lore]]**|Monopolize rare knowledge, profit from it, gain access to the Liber Infinitus|The book exists. Janussi is their inside contact. Not that Xanthoria is a lich or controls Janussi.|
|**[[Xanthoria]]**|Obtain the Liber Infinitus, perfect her research into true dominion over life and death|Everything. She created this entire situation.|
|**[[The Avowed]] / Candlekeep**|Protect knowledge, maintain the keep, serve scholars|Almost nothing. They do not know the Keepers exist, or that Janussi is compromised.|

> [!note] The Key Dramatic Irony The Keepers believe they are the architects of a brilliant conspiracy. They are not. They are Xanthoria's pawns — a layer of human ambition she is using as insulation between herself and Candlekeep. When she is done with them, she will infect them all.

## The Villain — Xanthoria

Xanthoria was once a half-elf druid of Silvanus whose forest home was threatened by undead. Her research into fungi and lichen was originally noble — she wanted to protect her forest. That research gradually twisted, influenced by the demon queen Zuggtmoy, until Xanthoria turned her experiments on herself and transformed into a lichen lich.

She sits on the inner council of the Keepers of Forbidden Lore. They do not know what she is. They know her as a brilliant, reclusive scholar with unusual resources and an uncanny ability to gather information. She has been patient. She has been planning this for a very long time.

> [!important] What the Other Keepers Don't Know
> 
> - Xanthoria is a lichen lich
> - She is the one who infected Janussi and the other Avowed
> - She plans to infect every Keeper council member in time
> - She is using the entire organization as a disposable tool

**Her phylactery:** A living sprite named Thunderwing. As long as Thunderwing lives, Xanthoria cannot be permanently destroyed. The party must discover this before the final fight — or their victory will be temporary.

## The Saprophytic Plague — Campaign Mechanic

The plague is not just a disease. It is Xanthoria's long reach.

Infected humanoids develop a mycelial psychic connection to Xanthoria over time. Early infection produces physical symptoms — body aches, nausea, exhaustion. But the true effect is cognitive: small thoughts that don't feel like their own. Preferences that shift. Decisions that seem reasonable but serve Xanthoria's agenda. Eventually, the infected become soft puppets — still appearing functional, still believing themselves autonomous, while their will is quietly eroded.

In advanced stages, infected creatures drone the word _"Xanthoria"_ over and over before death claims them.

> [!warning] Janussi's Progression Track how far along Janussi is as the campaign progresses. Early sessions — occasional odd decisions, a meeting cancelled, a bounty redirected. Mid campaign — more pronounced strangeness, scholars going quiet, the party's access to certain areas becoming subtly restricted. Late campaign — Janussi is nearly fully compromised. The moment of recognition should feel like a punch.

**Curing the plague:** Any magic that cures a disease can rid an individual of the saprophytic plague. However, curing Janussi and the Avowed en masse requires a wider solution — likely tied to dealing with Xanthoria directly or destroying the mycelial network at its source.

## Act Structure — When Acts Break

Acts are defined by a combination of level progression and narrative milestones. Use the level ranges as a loose guide, but let the story moments define where each act actually closes. Don't force a break at a level — wait for the right scene.

|Act|Level Range|Ends When...|
|---|---|---|
|**Act One**|Levels 1–5|The party discovers the Keepers' safe house — the conspiracy becomes real and named|
|**Act Two**|Levels 6–10|The party confirms Janussi is compromised — the gut punch that reframes everything|
|**Act Three**|Levels 11–16|Xanthoria is defeated and the Liber Infinitus is secured — the campaign ends|

> [!note] Pacing Note Pell approaches the party during Act One. Thessaly surfaces during Act Two. Caelix arrives in Act Three. Each informant's arrival signals the stakes are rising — use them as act indicators as much as the level ranges.

## The Party's Journey — Four Acts

### Act One — The Mysteries

_Early sessions. The party runs bounties. Nothing seems connected yet._

- The party takes jobs from [[Brother Alec]] via the Avowed
- Each mystery is standalone — but every one has the Keepers' fingerprints somewhere
- Three quiet threads of unease begin simultaneously:
    - **Brother Alec** starts noticing patterns across the bounties — books missing, witnesses going quiet, a recurring symbol he can't place
    - **Janussi** has small, dismissible moments of strangeness — a cancelled meeting, an odd comment, a decision that seems slightly off
    - **A lower-ranking Avowed** (to be named) grows frightened and eventually approaches the party privately
- The party does not connect these threads yet. They are simply doing their jobs.

> [!note] DM Pacing Note Resist the urge to make the breadcrumbs obvious. Each thread should feel like nothing on its own. The satisfaction comes when the party connects them — not when you connect them for the party.

### Act Two — The Keepers Revealed

_Mid campaign. The conspiracy has a name._

- The party discovers **physical evidence** of the Keepers — documents bearing a recurring seal, a safe house, correspondence that reveals an organized operation
- They now know there is a shadowy organization operating against Candlekeep
- The three breadcrumb threads begin to converge — Brother Alec's pattern, the frightened Avowed's testimony, and the evidence all point in the same direction
- The Keepers become aware the party is getting close and begin actively working against them
- Xanthoria watches from a distance. She is not concerned. Yet.

> [!note] The Safe House The safe house should be somewhere plausible inside or very close to Candlekeep. Consider a location the party has already been to — a room in the House of Rest, a cellar beneath a merchant's cart in the Court of Air, a demiplanar chamber off The Hearth. Familiar made sinister.

### Act Three — The Plague Uncovered

_Late campaign. The real enemy comes into focus._

- The party discovers the Saprophytic Plague is involved — either through Nidalia's suspicions, the frightened Avowed showing symptoms, or their own investigation
- They trace the plague's origin back to the Lykortha Expanse
- They discover that Xanthoria — one of the Keepers' inner council — is a lichen lich
- They discover Janussi is compromised — this should land as a late, gut-punch revelation
- The party must find a way to cure the plague before they can face Xanthoria with any hope of freeing Janussi and the other Avowed
- Curing the plague weakens Xanthoria's hold and frees her puppets

> [!important] The Janussi Revelation This needs to be earned. If the party has come to trust Janussi — even like him — the moment they realise what has been done to him reframes everything. Don't rush it. Let it be late. Let it hurt.

### Act Four — The Climax

_Final sessions. Candlekeep's heart is at stake._

- The plague is cured. Xanthoria's hold on the Avowed is broken.
- Janussi, freed and himself again, becomes a powerful ally. He understands what the Keepers wanted and what Xanthoria truly is.
- Xanthoria reveals herself fully. She corrupts **the Great Library** — Candlekeep's heart — with the Saprophytic Plague, transforming it into a fungal nightmare of overgrown shelves, mycelial tendrils, and spore-filled air.
- The final confrontation takes place inside the corrupted Great Library.
- Janussi, now free, commands **Miirym** — who fights alongside the party against Xanthoria.
- The party must discover and deal with **Thunderwing** (Xanthoria's phylactery) to permanently end her.
- Victory secures the Liber Infinitus. What they do with it is their choice.

> [!warning] Thunderwing The party needs to discover that Xanthoria cannot be permanently killed without dealing with her phylactery. This should not be handed to them — they should find this out through investigation, a dying Keeper's confession, Nidalia's warning, or Janussi's knowledge. Destroying Thunderwing is a moral weight — she is an innocent sprite. That should feel like something.

## The Three Breadcrumb Threads

### Thread 1 — Brother Alec

[[Brother Alec]] is the party's day-to-day contact with the Avowed. He assigns bounties, checks in, debriefs them after missions. He is in a unique position to notice patterns across jobs — and he does.

- Early: He mentions offhandedly that this is the third bounty in a row involving a book that came from the same district of Baldur's Gate
- Mid: He comes to the party quietly and says he flagged a concern with Janussi — and Janussi dismissed it unusually abruptly. That bothered him.
- Late: He has compiled a list. He brings it to the party. He is scared.

### Thread 2 — Janussi

The party will encounter [[Janussi]] as a formal authority figure — dignified, learned, kind in the way powerful people can afford to be. The strangeness should be barely perceptible at first.

- Early: A decision that seems slightly bureaucratic — a restriction on a part of the library the party needed, explained away reasonably
- Mid: A moment of blankness mid-conversation. He recovers smoothly. Nobody mentions it.
- Late: He says something that, in retrospect, could only have been said by someone working against Candlekeep's interests. The party may not catch it in the moment. They will remember it later.

### Thread 3 — The Frightened Avowed

A lower-ranking Avowed member (name TBD) has noticed things going wrong and has no idea who to trust inside Candlekeep. They approach the party because the party are outsiders — not Avowed, not compromised.

- Early: They pass a note or find a moment alone. Vague fears. They don't know what they've seen.
- Mid: They have more. A name they overheard. A room they weren't supposed to find.
- Late: They go quiet suddenly — either because they've been compromised, silenced, or because they've found something that truly frightened them.

## The Physical Evidence Moment

The party finds the Keepers' safe house. This is the scene that transforms abstract suspicion into concrete knowledge — they now have a name, a symbol, and proof.

**What they find:**

- Documents bearing the Keepers of Forbidden Lore seal — correspondence between members
- A list of target texts — books the Keepers have already extracted or are trying to access
- References to an inside contact at Candlekeep (Janussi is not named — referred to as _"the Keeper"_)
- A recurring symbol — to be designed, used as the Keepers' identifier going forward
- Possibly: a reference to _"the prize beneath the keep"_ without naming the Liber Infinitus

> [!note] Location Suggestion The safe house should be somewhere the party has already been. A place made sinister in retrospect. Consider a demiplanar chamber off The Hearth, or a cellar in the Court of Air.

## DM Notes — Things to Remember

> [!important] Pacing Rules
> 
> - The Keepers' fingerprints appear in **every mystery** — but never obviously. Always deniable.
> - Janussi's strangeness should be **barely perceptible** until it's too late to dismiss
> - Nidalia is the party's oracle — she suspects something is wrong at Candlekeep and will share fragments as they bring her information. She does not know about the plague yet.
> - The Liber Infinitus should feel like a revelation when named — not a premise

> [!warning] The Moral Weight of the Ending Three things in the climax carry moral weight that should not be rushed past:
> 
> 1. **Janussi's freedom** — he is a good man who was hollowed out. His return to himself should be a moment.
> 2. **Thunderwing** — an innocent sprite who is Xanthoria's phylactery. Destroying her ends the lich but costs something.
> 3. **The Liber Infinitus** — the party now holds a book that could unmake the world. What do they do with it?

> [!note] Character Arc Tie-Ins
> 
> - **Lucifurry** — Bhaal's interest in him is separate from the main arc, but the Shadowfell and undead themes of Xanthoria's story rhyme with it. Let them echo without forcing a connection.
> - **Morvax** — His whispering relics and hunger for power make the Liber Infinitus personally dangerous for him. Watch how Kade plays the moment they find the book.
> - **Vyr'ashen (Ash)** — His full moon vulnerability and hidden past could be leveraged by the Keepers if they discover it. A good pressure point mid-campaign.

# Session Prep — Book of the Raven

_Starting: After the village battle | Adventure: Book of the Raven | Act One_

---

## Where We Are

The party has just finished a battle in the abandoned village (Wytchway). They have the map. The chalet is ahead — a slog up a lonely hill, fog thickening around them as they climb. They arrive at Chalet Brantifax.

---

## The Road — Toothsome Enters

> [!important] New Player Introduction — Birhan's First Session
> Toothsome meets the party on the road between Wytchway and Chalet Brantifax. He is heading to the same destination for a completely different reason — and he doesn't know who hired him.

The party is still on the road, the chalet not yet visible through the fog. A lone figure comes into view ahead — a tiefling, male, traveling light. Shield on his back, mace at his hip. A red-and-yellow symbol on his chest: a flaming sword on a shield. Tempus.

**He is heading to the same place they are.**

**What Toothsome has on him:**
- A bag of 50 gp (already received)
- An anonymous note (give Birhan the printed prop)

**What the note says:** 
> *A figurine, carved black, the size of a fist. It rests beneath rubble in the upper loft of Chalet Brantifax — a highland estate two days north of Wytchway. You will know it by smell before you see it.*
>
> *Take it. Tell no one. Leave it wrapped in cloth at the base of the broken fountain in Rivington, south Baldur's Gate, before the next new moon.*
>
> *A second bag — three times this one — will be waiting beneath the stone.*
>
> *Ask no questions. We are watching to see that you don't.*

**What Toothsome does NOT know:**
- The Keepers of Forbidden Lore hired him via the anonymous note
- The figurine's intended purpose is to neutralise Miirym — a spectral dragon guardian in Candlekeep's catacombs
- A previous Keeper agent already searched C14 and left empty-handed (the wereravens secured it first)
- He is unknowingly working for the campaign's main villains

> [!note] How to Play the Meeting
> Toothsome is Steel Fang — he travels openly, not suspiciously. He has no reason to hide that he's going to the chalet, only why. If asked, he can truthfully say he has business there. Let Birhan decide how much Toothsome volunteers. The tension is dramatic irony — the table doesn't need to know either.

> [!warning] Conflict Point — C14 Baron's Loft
> Both the party and Toothsome are heading toward the same room for the Orcus Figurine. His job is to steal it. Their job is to explore the chalet. How this resolves depends entirely on what happens when they find it — and whether Toothsome has told them about the job by then. Don't force it. Let it land.

**If the party asks why he's traveling alone:**
Steel Fang operate alone or in small, precise units. This is normal for him.

**Tempus passphrase** (if relevant — another Tempus worshipper present):
*"Tempus thanks you"* / *"And I thank Tempus."*

## Arrival — Read Aloud

> [!readaloud] The hill is steeper than the map suggests. Before you see anything, the smell of rotten meat stinks your nose. By the time the chalet comes into view, the fog has swallowed everything below you. The building is stone — three stories, a tower rising from one end, clay roof tiles dark with age and moisture. Ravens line every surface. Dozens of them. They don't flee as you approach. They just watch. When night falls, the ravens fly off without warning — all of them at once, a black wave lifting from the chalet and disappearing into the dark. An eerie silence settles over the hill. Then you see lights moving inside.

## Phase 1 — The Chalet (Exploration)

The wereravens are in **C14 (Baron's Loft)**. They know the party is here. They will use mimicry to scare the party off before making direct contact. Roll d6 for mimicry source if needed:

|d6|Sound|Appears to come from|
|---|---|---|
|1|Glass breaking (Madrina)|Servants' attic (C16)|
|2|Snorting hog (Taspar)|Kitchen (C7)|
|3|Creaking door (Rennick)|Guest room (C10 or C11)|
|4|Body being dragged (Rennick)|Directly above or below party|
|5|Laughing child (Vinique)|Attic nursery (C15)|
|6|Dying man's scream (Vinique)|Storage cellar (C1)|

**When they can no longer hide:** Wereravens come down in humanoid form — black robes, scarlet sashes — and assure the party they mean no harm. They assume the party is here to loot or to use the shadow crossing. Tell them there's no treasure. If the party acts threatening, they shift to raven form and flee permanently.

## Key Rooms — What Matters

**C1 Storage Cellar:** Empty crates. The door to C3 swings open on its own. Characters with Passive Perception 14+ hear whispering: _"I can't get out."_

**C3 Haunted Well** ⭐ BHAAL HOOK 2 The well whispers: _"Brorn! Where are you, boy?"_ — Baron's ghost calling for his dead mastiff. See Bhaal Hook 2 below.

- Well shaft: 60 ft down to a flooded cistern. DC 11 Athletics to climb.
- Treasure in cistern: holy symbol of Sune on a gold chain (25 gp). If reunited with the baron's grave (C9), each character gains a charm of heroism.

**C4 Cloakroom:** Shovel and rake on hooks. Dusty cloak — pocket contains a rusty iron key (opens padlock in C16). Two moths fly out of the cloak if disturbed.

**C5 Den:** Hunting trophies. Nothing valuable. Staircase up to C8.

**C8 Parlor:** Gold-inlaid table (heavy, 300+ lbs), gilded harp (heavy). Wereravens removed dust sheets here — signs of use.

**C9 Graveyard** ⭐ BHAAL HOOK 1 See Bhaal Hook 1 below. Four graves: Baron Brantifax, BRORN (mastiff), HELUTHE (_Lost Too Soon_), SYLPHENE (_May She Find Peace at Last_).

- Heluthe's grave: DO NOT DIG. Contains a scarecrow and two crawling claws.
- Sylphene's grave: The shadow crossing. Opening it at night activates the gate to the Shadowfell.
- Baron's grave: Buried with gold signet ring (25 gp, stag silhouette).

**C12 Study:** Rolltop desk (locked, DC 13 Dex with thieves' tools). Contains calligrapher's supplies and a leather journal — baroness's diary. One hour reading reveals family history including Sylphene's deformities and death and Heluthe's death by wolf.

**C13 Master Bedroom:** The wereravens sleep here. Twist the bedpost nearest the south wall counterclockwise to open the secret door to C14.

**C14 Baron's Loft** ⭐ BHAAL HOOK 3 + KEEPER FINGERPRINT See Bhaal Hook 3 below and Keeper Fingerprint below.

- Explosion destroyed the upper tower stairs. Alchemist's supplies in the wreckage (worthless).
- Upper level (not visible from below): unlocked wooden chest with scorched lid. Contains **potion of mind reading** (imbiber's skin sparkles for duration) and 6 random trinkets. Wereravens don't care if the party takes the potion.
- Wereravens will bury the Orcus Figurine under rubble here once they decide it's the safest hiding spot.

**C15 Attic Nursery** ⭐ BHAAL HOOK 4 See Bhaal Hook 4 below. Poltergeist of Sylphene activates when dolls on the shelf are disturbed. Uses specter stat block with poltergeist variant.

**C16 Servants' Attic:** Stuck door (DC 13 Str). Two holes in roof — wereraven entry/exit. Padlocked trunk (key from C4 or DC 12 Dex): studded leather armor (portly adult human), leather helm, heavy crossbow, 15 crossbow bolts (crimson fletching), two hunting traps. Wardrobes: 5 dusty black robes, 5 pairs sandals, 5 scarlet sashes.

**C17 Storage Attic:** Two holes in roof. Six canvas-wrapped objects: 2 gilt mirrors (1 cracked, 1 worth 50 gp), 4 oil paintings (50 gp each). One painting: Baron and baroness portrait — stout man in his 50s, tall thin woman, gray mastiff at their feet.

## The Four Bhaal Hooks

> [!important] DM Philosophy Bhaal does not announce himself. Everything is deniable. Keep all hooks private to Luci's player. The other party members should notice something is slightly off about Luci over time — but never be able to pin it down.

---

### Hook 1 — The Warm Grave (C9 Graveyard)

**Trigger:** First time Luci steps near or onto Baron Brantifax's grave.

The adventure text says any character who stands on Baron Brantifax's grave "feels a harmless chill run through them."

**Run it like this:** Describe the chill to Morvax and Ash normally. Then privately to Luci's player: he feels warmth instead. Not heat — warmth. Like stepping into a firelit room after a cold walk. And the unmistakable feeling of being **watched and approved of**. No voice. No figure. Just that. It stops the moment he steps off the grave.

- [x] Triggered

---

### Hook 2 — Sister's Voice (C3 Haunted Well)

**Trigger:** When Luci passes within earshot of the well.

The well whispers _"Brorn! Where are you, boy?"_ to the whole party.

**Run it like this:** Every party member hears the man whisper. Then — quieter, layered beneath — Luci alone hears a second voice. A girl. Just his name. Once. Then silence. It is unmistakably his sister's voice. The moment he tries to focus on it or move toward it, it's gone. Do not confirm or deny what he heard if he asks the party. Nobody else caught it.

- [x] Triggered

---

### Hook 3 — The Smell (C14 Baron's Loft)

**Trigger:** When Luci comes within 5 feet of the Orcus Figurine.

The figurine smells of decaying flesh, detectable within 5 feet.

**Run it like this:** Describe the smell clearly to the party — rotting, sweet-sick, wrong. Watch for reactions. Do NOT prompt Luci's player. If his player doesn't volunteer a reaction, quietly ask: _"What does Luci notice about the smell?"_ The answer is that he doesn't react. It doesn't register as unpleasant. If pressed, he has to actually think about it and realise with quiet horror that it smells almost... familiar. Like something from a dream he can't quite place. Do not over-explain. Drop it.

- [ ] Triggered

---

### Hook 4 — The Laugh (C15 Attic Nursery)

**Trigger:** When Luci enters the nursery.

Sylphene's poltergeist manifests when the dolls are disturbed. It normally tries to frighten away intruders — rattling, shrieking, hostility.

**Run it like this:** When Morvax and Ash enter, run the poltergeist as normal — unsettling, aggressive. When Luci enters or is already inside, the poltergeist goes **completely still**. No rattling. No shrieking. It simply stares in his direction. Then, once — softly, in the voice of a small girl — the room makes the sound of a child laughing. A specific laugh. Luci recognises it. The poltergeist does not attack Luci.

- [x] Triggered

## Keeper Fingerprint — C14 Baron's Loft

A Keeper agent visited Chalet Brantifax recently looking for the Orcus Figurine. They searched C14 (where it's rumoured to be hidden) but the wereravens had already secured it. They left in a hurry.

**Plant one of these in C14 — your choice:**

- Boot prints in the soot that don't match any wereraven's feet (DC 17 Survival or Investigation)
- A torn scrap of parchment caught under a floorboard: _"...spectral guardian cannot be moved by..."_ — rest missing (DC 20 Investigation)
- A crowbar left behind that clearly doesn't belong (DC 13 Investigation)

Don't draw attention to it. Let the party find it or not. If they do, they don't know what it means yet.

## Phase 2 — Meeting the Wereravens

When revealed, they come down in humanoid form. Madrina speaks first — calm, matter-of-fact. Taspar hovers nearby, nervous. Rennick is watchful. Vinique has the figurine on her person (in an old, nonfunctional lantern she keeps at her side).

**What the wereravens will share if the party is friendly:**

- They're the Scarlet Sash — they take evil items out of circulation
- Vinique stole the Orcus Figurine from an evil merchant before it could be auctioned
- They're debating the best place to hide it (they'll bury it in C14 under rubble)
- The shadow crossing in the graveyard — Sylphene's grave, only at night
- They're happy to explain how the crossing works if the party has earned their trust

**Joining the Scarlet Sash:** Only wereravens can join formally. But earning their trust is possible and worth pursuing — they're the party's best potential Act One ally.

---

## Phase 3 — The Shadow Crossing (Shadowfell Necropolis)

**To activate:** Open Sylphene's grave (shovel from C4). At night, fog fills the grave. Lie down in it → transported to the Shadowfell.

**The Shadowfell side:** A matching burial site in the middle of a circular necropolis, 300 ft diameter. Open graves, tilted bare headstones, plundered mausoleums.

**Encounter — waves:**

**Wave 1: Gargoyles** Two gargoyles roost atop the Harrn Mausoleum, 30 ft north of the shadow crossing. They have an unobstructed view of the entire necropolis. They attack immediately.

**Wave 2: Ghouls (trigger: gargoyles dead OR party moves 30+ ft from crossing)** 12 ghouls hiding in open graves. They approach in 4 groups of 3, from each cardinal direction simultaneously.

**Wave 3: Drovath Harrn (joins ghouls)** Wight. Inhabits the Harrn Mausoleum. Wears **ring of jumping** on his right hand — uses it to leap onto mausoleum rooftops and attack with his longbow from elevation.

> [!note] Harrn Mausoleum Interior Central chamber: skulls and bones of 3 warhorses. Two sarcophagi closed (inanimate human bones). One open — Drovath's, empty. Hidden compartment in open sarcophagus base: DC 13 Perception. Contains Drovath's **saddle of the cavalier**. WARNING: If anyone other than Drovath removes the saddle from the mausoleum, the 3 warhorse skeletons animate as warhorse skeletons and attack the thief.

---

### Hook 5 — First Bloodthirst (Shadowfell Necropolis) ⭐

**Trigger:** The very first time Luci's Bloodthirst feature activates in combat.

When a ghoul hits the Bloodied threshold (drops to half HP but isn't killed), before Luci's player decides to use the reaction:

_"Luci — your body is already moving before your mind catches up. You're across the battlefield, blade in hand, standing over the ghoul before you registered the choice."_

Then let the player make the attack roll. Afterward, quietly ask: _"Is Luci disturbed by that... or did it feel right?"_ Let that question sit unanswered. Don't rush past it.

- [ ] Triggered

---

### Hook 6 — Drovath's Whisper ⭐

**Trigger:** The moment Drovath Harrn is reduced to 0 HP.

Describe the death normally to the party. Then, privately to Luci's player only, beneath the sound of combat and crumbling bone:

_"He sees you, child. He has always seen you."_

Nobody else hears it. If Luci tells the party, nobody can confirm it happened.

- [ ] Triggered

---

## Returning to the Material Plane

The shadow crossing returns to Chalet Brantifax's graveyard — but **only in the gloomy hours of the morning**. If the party comes back and the wereravens have had time, they may have filled in Sylphene's grave. A character trying to use the crossing to get back may emerge buried under six feet of earth — DC 13 Athletics to crawl out. Darkly funny if timed right.

If the wereravens are friendly, they're eager to hear what happened in the Shadowfell. This is a good moment to deepen the relationship with the Scarlet Sash and set up the next adventure hook.

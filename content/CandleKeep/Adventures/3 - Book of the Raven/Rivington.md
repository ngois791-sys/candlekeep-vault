## The Rivington Drop — Read Aloud

> [!readaloud] Arriving in Rivington
> Rivington doesn't announce itself. It just appears — the city thinning out around you until the packed streets of Baldur's Gate give way to something older and quieter. The buildings here are lower, the lanes narrower, the smell of the harbour closer than you'd like. Washing lines stretch between windows. A dog watches you from a doorway and doesn't bother to bark. This is the kind of neighbourhood that minds its own business and expects you to do the same.

> [!readaloud] Finding the Fountain
> The fountain is where the note said it would be — at the bend where the lane widens into a small forgotten square. It must have been something once. The basin is cracked across the middle, one side sunken lower than the other, filled now with black water and a winter's worth of dead leaves. A carved figure still stands at its centre — a woman, one arm broken off at the elbow, her face worn smooth by rain and time. She holds nothing. Whatever she was offering is long gone. At the base of the fountain, half buried under a loose stone, the gap is exactly large enough for a bag.

> [!readaloud] The Drop
> The figurine is heavier than it looks. You unwrap it from the cloth — the smell hits you again, faint and sweet and wrong — and set it down in the gap at the base of the stone. The moment your hand leaves it, you feel it. A low hum, like a bowstring just after release. Then the stone around the figurine lights up. Not fire. Not light exactly. A sigil — precise, deliberate, already complete — burns white for half a second and then is gone. So is the figurine. The cloth is still there. The smell is not.

> [!readaloud] The Payment
> Where the figurine was, there is now a bag. Leather, tied with a plain cord. No note. No mark. No sound from anywhere in the square. The woman in the fountain stares at nothing with her worn, featureless face. Somewhere nearby, a shutter closes. You are alone. The job is done, and whoever hired you has no further interest in you at all.

> [!note] DM Note
> Let the silence after the last line sit for a beat before asking Birhan what Toothsome does next. The emptiness is the point — he did something significant for someone powerful, and they didn't even bother to acknowledge it. That should feel just slightly wrong to him, even if he can't name why.

## Description
**Smithy and Stables** is a combined forge and mount house run by **Khe’ril Hammerbind**, a gold dwarf veteran known for his skill and steady temper. The front half of the building is the smithy, warm from the forge and filled with the steady sounds of hammering metal. Khe’ril and his apprentices shape horseshoes and metal fittings here, the smell of hot iron always lingering in the air. Tools hang neatly on the walls, and a sturdy anvil sits at the center of the workspace.

A wide doorway connects the smithy to the stables. The warmth from the forge keeps the stables comfortable in colder months. The first rows of stalls are built for horses, ponies, and mules, each simple but well-kept. Farther back are the reinforced stalls designed for larger and more unusual mounts like hippogriffs, griffons, and wyverns. These stalls are taller, sturdier, and fitted with stronger latches to keep the creatures secure and calm.

The whole place carries a mix of scents—warm metal, hay, leather, and animals. Despite the variety of creatures housed inside, Smithy and Stables is orderly, practical, and clearly run with care.

> [!readaloud]  
> As you step inside Smithy and Stables, the smell of warm metal and fresh hay hits you immediately. To your left, a stocky gold dwarf hammers a glowing horseshoe on an anvil, sparks jumping with every strike. This is **Khe’ril Hammerbind**, the veteran dwarf who runs the place, his forge filling the front room with steady heat. Along the walls of the smithy, you spot racks of freshly crafted weapons—simple but sturdy blades, short spears, reinforced maces, and a few finely made axes with polished edges. Some are wrapped and ready for pickup; others hang openly, still cooling from the forge. A few unfinished pieces lie on a workbench, tools arranged neatly beside them. Through an open doorway, the stables stretch back into the building. Rows of clean stalls hold horses and ponies, their hooves resting on fresh straw. Farther in, larger reinforced pens house mounts meant for the skies—hippogriffs, griffons, and even wyverns. You hear hooves thudding, wings rustling, and the occasional low screech echoing from the back. The whole place feels busy but well-kept—part armory, part workshop, part stable—and clearly maintained by skilled, experienced hands.

> [!note]+ Khe'ril Hammerbind  
> **Race:** Hill Dwarf
> 
> **Appearance:** Khe’ril is a broad-shouldered gold dwarf with fire-red hair and a thick, flame-colored beard, both braided with bits of copper wire to keep them out of the forge. His skin is weathered from heat and battle, and his eyes are a steady molten-gold, matching the sparks that often cling to his beard.
> 
> **Personality:** Khe’ril Hammerbind is a gold dwarf veteran with the personality of someone who’s worked hard his entire life but still cares deeply about the people—and creatures—around him. He speaks in a low, gravelly voice, always sounding a bit tired but never unfriendly. He’s straightforward and practical, the sort of dwarf who rarely wastes words. When he does speak, it’s usually blunt, honest, and to the point. Despite his stern appearance, he’s patient with newcomers and surprisingly gentle with the mounts in his care. He treats every creature—from a simple pony to a trained griffon—as if it’s worth his respect. He’s protective of his apprentices and takes pride in teaching them “the right way” to do things, even if he grumbles while doing it. Khe’ril doesn’t brag about his past as a soldier, but he carries himself with quiet confidence. Those who look closely can tell he’s seen real battles, yet he seems more at peace here in the steady rhythm of hammering and horses than he ever was on the battlefield. He’s happiest when the forge is glowing, the stalls are clean, and everyone is doing their job without fuss. If players speak to him, he’ll come off as gruff at first—but warm and dependable once they earn even a sliver of his trust.
> 
> **Voice Guide:** Khe’ril speaks in a low, rumbling dwarven tone—gritty but calm—with short, clipped sentences. He sounds like someone who’s spent years shouting over a forge: rough-edged, steady, and always a little out of breath, but with surprising warmth when talking about mounts or metalwork.

## Description

Erudite Outfitters & Clothiers is a modest but essential shop within Candlekeep, known for crafting robes, travel attire, and finely made protective gear for scholars and adventurers alike. Its warm interior carries the scents of leather, silk, and herbal tea, with neatly organized sections displaying robes, reinforced leathers, durable travel cloaks, and select pieces of custom-forged armor. The atmosphere is calm, orderly, and subtly infused with Feldmar’s meticulous craftsmanship.

The shop is run by **Feldmar Bisset III**, a patient human [[priest-acolyte-xmm|Priest Acolyte]] of Gond and master tailor whose family earned a respected place among the Avowed through generations of textile expertise. **Feldmar Bisset III** is a tidy, soft-spoken human in his late forties, with neatly trimmed dark hair and a pair of round spectacles that constantly slide down his nose. His clothes are clean, practical, and carefully stitched, clearly made by his own hand. A faint smell of silk, tea, and fresh linen seems to follow him wherever he goes. Feldmar teaches all adjutants a mandatory course in simple stitching to reduce patchwork demands and instill an appreciation for practical care of clothing. His mechanical flair, combined with traditional tailoring, allows him to craft garments that are both functional and elegantly understated.

Beside the workshop lies Feldmar’s cherished **silk atrium**, where rows of mulberry plants support his silkworm colonies. From their cocoons, he harvests small amounts of rare, exceptionally smooth silk that he incorporates into masterwork robes, specialized cloaks, and select pieces of lightweight armor. These silk-infused items—comfortable, resilient, and subtly enchanted—are highly sought after by the Avowed and visiting adventurers alike.

The shop’s expanded inventory includes leather and metal armor designed for mobility, field-ready cloaks, padded half-helms, and ceremonial headgear, all tailored with Candlekeep’s understated aesthetic. Whether someone needs a simple mend, a fresh robe for study, or specialized gear for a dangerous expedition, Erudite Outfitters remains a trusted and well-loved cornerstone of life in Candlekeep.

> [!tip]- Feldmar's Voice Guide
> **Tone and Mood**
> 	- Soft-spoken, calm, and gentle
> 	- Rarely sounds rushed or irritated
> 	- Warm, slightly distracted, like he's thinking about stitching patterns while talking
> **Pacing**
> 	- Speaks slow and deliberately
> 	- Pause to think before answering
> 	- Occasionally trails of into a quiet "Hmmm..." before finishing a thought
> **Speech Patterns**
> 	- Uses precise, careful language
> 	- Avoids contractions (I am instead of I'm)
> 	- Compliments good craftmanship whenever he notices it
> **Example Phrases**
> 	- "Please, take your time. Rushing only leads to crooked seams."
> 	- "Silk has its own personality, you see. It wishes to be guided, not forced."
> 	- "You adventuring sorts do keep me busy, but it is more fun to make these items rather than just simple robes."

## Items for Sale

- **Normal armor, helmets, cloaks, and other wearables.**


### **Helm of Quiet Clarity** (65 gp)
- This lightweight half-helm is lined with Feldmar’s silken padding, which muffles sound and fits the head like a gentle embrace.
- **Properties:**
	- **Focused Mind:** You have advantage on **Constitution saving throws made to maintain concentration** on a spell _once per short rest_.
	- **Whisper-Muffling:** Your footsteps and minor movements are unusually soft; you gain a **+1 bonus to Stealth checks involving quiet movement**.
- The helm is favored by researchers delving into dusty vaults where both silence and concentration are valuable assets.

### **Silkwoven Leather (Armor, Light Armor, Uncommon, Requires Attunement)** (180 gp)
- This supple set of armor is reinforced with panels of Feldmar’s rare hand-harvested silk, creating a protective but unbelievably flexible suit.
- **Properties:**
	- **AC as Studded Leather (12 + Dex).**
    - **Silken Resilience:** Once per long rest, when you take damage, you may reduce that damage by **1d4** as the silk fibers stiffen kinetically.
    - **Unrestricted Motion:** You have **advantage on Acrobatics checks** to escape restraints, slip through tight spaces, or maintain footing.
- Balanced for level 2: modest defensive use, useful skill benefit, and no stacking AC boosts.

### **Cloak of the Gentle Wind (Wondrous Item, Common)** (55 gp)
- A lightweight travel cloak that seems to flutter even when the air is still.
- **Properties:**
	- **Soft Descent:** When you fall, the first **20 feet** of the fall deal no damage as the cloak balloons outward.
    - **Wind-Tuned:** You gain a **+1 bonus to Survival checks related to weather** or navigating strong winds.
- Perfect for travelers along the windy cliffs surrounding Candlekeep.

### **Ink-Shadow Cloak (Wondrous Item, Common)** (75 gp)
- This deep charcoal cloak is woven with silk that absorbs light like wet ink.
- **Properties:**
	- **Dim-Blend:** While you remain motionless in dim light or darkness, you gain a **+2 bonus to Stealth checks**.
    - **Shadow Veil:** Once per short rest, when you are in dim light or darkness, you can activate the cloak as a **free action** to lightly blur your outline. For the next **minute**, creatures have **disadvantage on Perception checks** made to spot you _visually_.
- A favorite among skulking scholars on secret errands.

### **Cloak of Minor Deflection (Wondrous Item, Uncommon, Requires Attunement)** (160 gp)
- A dusky blue cloak subtly threaded with faint silver runes; the silk responds to sudden movement by stiffening momentarily.
- **Properties:**
	- **Reactive Weave:** When you are hit by a ranged weapon attack, you may use your reaction to gain **+2 AC against that attack**, potentially causing it to miss.  
    (Usable **once per short rest**.)
    - **Silken Rebalance:** When you are forced to make a **Dexterity saving throw**, you may add a **+1 bonus** to the roll as the cloak subtly adjusts your center of gravity.  _(No cooldown; passive.)_
- A level-2 friendly form of minor ranged defense, often gifted to adventurers escorting scribes or researchers outside Candlekeep.

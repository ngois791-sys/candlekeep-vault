## Description of The Cart of Glittering Paths
Tibbin’s cart is a whimsical, portable workshop of polished cherrywood, no wider than a horse-drawn vegetable stall and pushed on sturdy brass-shod wheels. However, the cart’s construction is subtly arcane—its joints hum with the soft vibration of bound elemental energy, allowing it to move far more easily than its packed contents should permit. Tiny etched runes glow faintly when the cart is in motion, especially at dusk when Candlekeep’s shadows lengthen over the Court of Air.

The canopy is made from iridescent patchwork silk that shifts colors slowly—sometimes gold, sometimes azure, sometimes a shimmering peach tone—depending on the angle of the sun. Little crystal charms hang from the canopy edge, chiming softly in patterns that resemble music but never repeat the same melody twice. Many scholars believe Tibbin enchanted them; Tibbin insists the crystals simply “like to sing.”

Shelves fold outward like petals when Tibbin parks the cart, revealing trays of rings, pendants, earrings, clasps, and a few oddities whose purpose isn’t immediately clear. Small drawers open with musical clicks when customers approach, presenting items Tibbin thinks may interest them. A floating glass orb—Tibbin’s ever-present assistant "Glimmer"—hovers near the cart, projecting soft light and occasionally reshaping into lenses to magnify the finer details of gemstones.

A tiny brass sign hangs on the front, its letters rearranging themselves every few hours. Sometimes it reads _Underbough’s Wonders_, sometimes _Twitchspark Trinkets_, and once it read _Please Don’t Touch the Sapphire Box Unless You’re Fireproof_—an incident Tibbin insists was the cart “learning humor.”


> [!readaloud]  
> A small cherrywood cart rolls into the Court of Air, its brass wheels clicking softly on the stone. Hanging from the edges is a collection of tiny glass charms that chime together with each shift of the breeze. The cart unfolds smoothly as the halfling pushes it into place. Shelves slide outward, revealing rows of rings, pendants, bracelets, and glowing magical trinkets. A floating glass orb drifts beside the cart, adjusting lights and nudging displays as if it is helping arrange the merchandise. The whole setup gives the impression of a miniature traveling jewelry shop shining with warm colors, soft lights, and a faint scent of cedar and gemstone dust.

## Description of Tibbin Underbough
**Tibbin Underbough** is a lightfoot halfling with a permanently wind-ruffled mane of sandy-brown curls and cheeks smudged constantly with metal dust from late-night tinkering. His bright hazel eyes dart excitedly between customers, glittering with both genuine curiosity and the thrill of a good bargain. Always smiling—sometimes too much—Tibbin has the energy of a hummingbird and the gentle manners of someone who was raised in a place where hospitality was sacred law. He tends to talk in fast, charming bursts, slipping into little rhymes when nervous or excited.

He’s dressed in a fitted vest of soft plum-colored suede, etched with tiny glyphs for luck (some real, some purely decorative). Rings line nearly every finger, each one mismatched in design but meaningful to him. His left ear holds a tiny mechanical earring shaped like a dragonfly; it flutters its wings whenever he lies, much to his annoyance. Tibbin came to Candlekeep after leaving a failing artificer-jeweler’s guild in Baldur’s Gate—his unique fusion of artistry and magical whimsy wasn’t “profitable” enough for his old masters. He now wanders with pride, determined to prove that joyful creations have just as much place in the world as weapons and wards.

Despite his playful demeanor, Tibbin is deeply sentimental. Every piece he sells represents something meaningful—either crafted for a specific emotion he once felt or inspired by someone he’s met. He also quietly takes notes on every customer, sketching their faces in a little leather journal for future “inspiration.” He dreams of one day founding a traveling guild of magical artisans whose purpose would be to “spark wonder wherever feet can wander.”

**Tibbin’s Personality Traits (Player-Facing Tone)**:
- **Friendly Curiosity:** Tibbin enjoys meeting new people and always wants to know what brought them to Candlekeep. He asks gentle questions and listens closely.
- **Easily Excited:** Magic fascinates him. If someone shows interest in his work, he lights up and becomes animated.
- **Softhearted:** Tibbin cares deeply about others and often reads moods well. He offers comfort or humor when someone seems stressed.
- **Creative and Scatterbrained:** He jumps between topics, loses his tools, and sometimes forgets what he was about to say. His floating orb assistant helps keep him focused.
- **Quiet Melancholy (Subtle):** Players may notice small hints of wistfulness in quiet moments, though Tibbin never volunteers the reason.

**Voice Guide for Tibbin:**
- **Tone:** Warm, light, and friendly. His voice carries a soft musical cadence, like someone who hums often and enjoys pleasant sounds.
- **Pace:** Fast when he gets excited. Slower and thoughtful when he speaks about something personal.
- **Volume:** Generally quiet to moderate. He is more enthusiastic than loud.
- **Mannerisms**  
	- Speaks with his hands  
	- Laughs softly when nervous  
	- Makes little “oh” and “hmm” sounds when thinking  
	- Sometimes talks to his floating orb as if it were a pet
- **Example Lines**  
	- “Oh, yes, look at this one. It is one of my favorites. Careful though, it likes to sparkle when happy.”  
	- “You have a good eye. Not everyone notices the little details.”  
	- “I think this piece would suit you. It feels right somehow.”

## Secret Rare Items

### The Heartstring Circlet
**Rarity:** Rare — **Attunement Required**
**Price (if he _ever_ sells it):** **1,800 gp** (but Tibbin prefers _promises_ over coin)

**Appearance:** A delicate circlet of interwoven platinum strands, each thread impossibly thin, each one humming faintly with emotional resonance. At the center rests a heart-shaped opal that swirls with colors that match the wearer’s emotional state. When touched, the opal emits a soft melodic vibration—never the same tune twice. Tibbin keeps this item wrapped in blue velvet inside a cedarwood box carved with halfling runes of memory and affection.

**Lore:** Tibbin crafted the Heartstring Circlet for someone he loved deeply—his former partner Aelthwyn, an elven empath specializing in healing magic. They parted ways after a tragedy that Tibbin refuses to discuss, and though the circlet was meant as a gift of reconciliation, it was never given. He now keeps it hidden, unsure whether to let it go or hold it close.

**Properties:**
**Emotional Attunement:** 
- You always know the emotional state (general mood) of any creature you can see within 30 ft.  
- If a creature within 15 ft. attempts to deceive you with words, glamour, or magic, you gain **advantage** on Insight checks against it.

**Heart-Resonance Shield (1/day):**  
- As a reaction when you take damage, you may activate the circlet and gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus × 3. The opal flashes with the color of your fear or determination.

**Shared Sorrow (1/day):**  
- You may touch a willing creature and choose to share emotional burden. For 10 minutes:  
	- When they take psychic damage, you take half instead.  
	- When you take psychic damage, they take half instead.  
- (This effect does **not** reduce total psychic damage—only redistributes it.)

This ability makes the circlet a profoundly intimate artifact, one that binds wearers through shared vulnerability and trust.

### The Astral Lumen Locket
**Rarity:** Rare — **Attunement Required**  
**Price:** **2,400 gp**, though Tibbin only sells it to those “whose stories reach upward”

**Appearance:** A star-shaped locket of dark electrum, inlaid with swirling runes that resemble constellations. When opened, the interior reveals a tiny floating mote of starlight—a fragment of astral radiance captured from the Sea of Night during a planar disturbance. The mote orbits slowly inside, like a firefly trapped in heaven.

**Lore:** Tibbin didn’t make this one. It _found_ him. During one of his travels outside Baldur’s Gate, a strange astral ripple washed over his camp. In the chaos, this locket fell from the sky and landed softly in his lap. It has resisted every attempt to be opened by anyone but Tibbin… until he finally realized the locket responds to wishes—quiet, honest ones whispered when the heart feels small. He believes the item is searching for its rightful wielder. Someone with potential to “burn bright someday.” He’ll only offer it to someone who shows genuine wonder or speaks honestly about their hopes.

**Properties:**
**Astral Guidance:**  
- The locket grants **+2 bonus on Wisdom (Survival)** and **Intelligence (Arcana)** checks made to navigate, identify magical phenomena, or interpret celestial signs.

**Starlight Pulse (1/day):**  
- As an action, you release a burst of astral radiance.  
	- All creatures of your choice within 20 ft. must make a DC 14 Con save or take **2d6 radiant damage** (half on success).  
	- Allies in the radius instead regain **1d6 hit points** as fragments of starlight knit their wounds.

**Celestial Step (1/day):**  
- You may teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. When you appear, faint star-motes swirl briefly around you before fading.

**Hidden Feature (Not listed on merchant labels)**
- If the wearer makes a heartfelt wish aloud while holding the locket, they may gain a one-time bonus determined by destiny (DM discretion). This can spark an entire plot arc.
